Lemon-Raspberry Sorbet Cups

Besides being sweet, easy and refreshing, this dessert makes 4 servings for just 3 PointsPlus value!

What youll need:

  • cup sugar
  • 2 cups of raspberries
  • cup water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 lemons
  • Mint for garnish

How to make em:

1)In a small saucepan, combine water and sugar. Then bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Boil for 1 minute, reduce heat and simmer for 5 minutes more. Remove from heat and let cool.

2) Meanwhile, place raspberries in a food processor. Chop lemons into two halves, scoop out their pulp and set aside.

3) Combine cooled syrup, raspberries and lemon juice in a medium bowl.

4) Pour mixture into a shallow metal pan and freeze halfway through, about 20 to 30 minutes.

5) Rake a fork or spoon through the partially frozen mixture so it does not freeze into a solid block; return pan to freezer and let freeze.

6) Before serving, run sorbet through a food processor or blender to obtain desired texture.

7) Scoop the sorbet into the halved, scooped-out lemons, garnish with mint and serve!

Savory 7 Brunch

Along with some black coffee, aKale and Bacon Frittata andBanana Chocolate-Chip Mini Muffins are a perfect 7 PointsPlus value brunch!

Banana Chocolate-Chip Mini Muffins

Bake a batch of thesebite-size treats (1 PointsPlus value each)and freeze the rest for later!

What youll need:

  • 1 cup of light vanilla yogurt
  • cup of fat-free skim milk
  • cup of uncooked quick oats
  • tsp. of vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 large banana, mashed
  • 1 cups of all-purpose flour
  • cup of unpacked brown sugar
  • 2 tsp. of baking powder
  • tsp. of baking soda
  • tsp. of table salt
  • cup of mini chocolate chips, divided

How to make em:

1)Preheat oven to 375F and coat 36 mini muffin holes with cooking spray (use three 12-hole pans).

2) In a large mixing bowl, combine yogurt, milk, oats, vanilla extract and egg. Set aside 5 minutes for oats to soften and then stir in banana.

3) In a separate bowl, stir together flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda and salt. Stir flour mixture into yogurt mixture to moisten ingredients (do not beat - the dough will be very thick). Save 1 tablespoon of chips for later, and stir in the rest.

4) Spoon batter by heaping tablespoons into prepared muffin holes; sprinkle each with a few reserved chocolate chips. Bake until golden brown and a tester inserted in center of a muffin comes out clean (about 15 to 18 minutes).

Kale and Bacon Frittata

Filling, savory and5 PointsPlus value a serving ( of the entire finished product), frittatas are excellent for any meal, but brunch especially.

What youll need:

  • 1 tsp. of olive oil
  • 2 slices of uncooked turkey bacon, chopped
  • 2 medium uncooked shallots, chopped
  • 6 cups of uncooked kale, chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 large egg whites
  • cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • tsp. of table salt

How to make it:

1) Preheat broiler. In an oven-safe skillet, heat oil over medium heat and then add bacon.
2) Cook, stirring frequently for about 5-7 minutes until crisp. Then drain bacon on paper towels and set aside.
3) Reduce heat to medium-low and add shallots. Cook while stirring frequently for about 2-3 minutes until softened.
4) Add a few handfuls of kale, tossing with tongs and adding more as it cooks down until kale is tender (3-5 minutes). Add a splash of water if skillet seems dry while kales cooking.
5) In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, egg whites, bacon, cheese and salt. Then pour over kale mixture and stir to evenly distribute ingredients.
6) Allow to cook undisturbed until set (about 3-5 minutes).
7) Transfer skillet to oven; broil until browned (30 seconds to a minute).

Easy Corn Salsa

Mix up some corn salsa for a fresh and easy addition to any summer BBQ! Yields 6 servings at 3 PointsPlus value each.

What youll need:

6 ears of cooked and husked corn

cup of thinly sliced green onions

cup of halved cherry tomatoes

2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil

2 tablespoons of fresh lime juice

Salt and freshly ground pepper to taste

How to make it:

1)Cut the corn kernels from the cob into a large bowl.

2) Add onions, tomatoes, olive oil, lime juice, salt and pepper to the bowl.

3) Stir to combine.

4) Cover and allow to sit for at least 15 minutes, so the flavors can mix together.

Watermelon Keg & Frozen Virgin Watermelon Margaritas

This watermelon keg is perfect forany summertime soiree. Try it foryour Memorial Day Weekend BBQ and mix up Frozen Virgin Watermelon Margaritas to add into it!

Watermelon Keg

What youll need:

  • 1 medium to large oval watermelon
  • Large bowl & spoon
  • Kitchen knife & cutting board
  • Apple corer
  • Spigot (can be found at a beer making supply company or taken out of a jug)

How to make it:

  1. After washing the melon, place it on its side on top of a cutting board. Cut off a 1/4-1/2 slice from both ends. Dont cut too deep into the white part of the rind!
  2. Hollow out the watermelon with a spoon or scoop, setting aside the watermelon pieces for the margaritas. Be sure to leave about 2 of watermelon flesh towards the bottom of the watermelon so the spigot can be placed.
  3. Use an apple corer to cut a circle for your spigot to fit through the watermelon rind, placing it 2-3 inches from the bottom of the watermelon.
  4. Screw your spigot into place.
  5. Place the keg on a saucer or plate and fill with Frozen Virgin Watermelon Margaritas (recipe below!)

Frozen Virgin Watermelon Margaritas

(yields 4 servings at 2 PointsPlus value a serving)

What youll need:

  • 4 cups of fresh watermelon (seeds removed)
  • 3 tbsp of fresh lime juice
  • 2 tbsp of sugar
  • 4 cups of ice cubes
  • 1/8 tsp of salt
  • of a medium fresh lime, cut into wedges for garnish

How to make it:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a blender (except the lime wedges for garnish).
  2. Puree until smooth.
  3. Pour into the keg!

Perfect 10 Meal

Pair the following Beef and Veggie Stir Fry with Veggie Fried Rice and a 2 PointsPlus value sweet tea for a deliciously filling 10 PointsPlus value meal!

Beef and Veggie Stir Fry

Skip the takeout and make a quicker, 5 PointsPlus value version of a classic Chinese dish! Each serving'sabout 1 cups.

What youll need:

2 Tbsp. of cornstarch

tsp. of table salt

pound of uncooked lean, trimmed sirloin beef (thinly sliced against the grain)

2 tsp. of canola oil

1 cup of reduced-sodium chicken broth

5 cups of uncooked broccoli florets

1 Tbsp. of fresh, minced ginger root

tsp. of red pepper flakes (or to taste!)

cup of water

cup of low-sodium soy sauce

How to make it:

1) On a plate, combine 2 tablespoons of cornstarch(save the other Tbsp. for later)and salt; add beef and toss to coat.

2)Heat oil in a large nonstick wok or large deep skillet over medium-high heat.

3) Add beef and stir-fry until lightly browned and cooked through (should be about 4 minutes); transfer to a bowl with a slotted spoon.

4)Add cup of broth to same pan (save the other half for later); stir to loosen any bits of food on bottom of pan.

5) Add broccoli. Cover and cook, tossing occasionally and sprinkling with a tablespoon of water if needed, until broccoli is almost crisp-tender (about 3 minutes).

6) Uncover pan and add ginger, garlic and red pepper flakes; stir-fry until fragrant (about 1 minute).

7)In a cup, stir together water, soy sauce, remaining cup of broth and remaining tablespoon of cornstarch until blended; stir into pan.

8) Reduce heat to medium-low and bring to a simmer until slightly thickened (about 1 minute).

9)Return beef and accumulated juices to pan; toss to coat.

So Easy Veggie Fried Rice

Leftover brown rice gets a makeover with this delicious 3 PointsPlus value recipe!Serving size is about cup.

What youll need:

2 spritzes of cooking spray

1 large egg, beaten

2 medium uncooked scallions, cut into 1-inch pieces

1 medium sweet red pepper, thinly sliced

1 cup of frozen peas and carrots

1 Tbsp. of minced garlic

1 Tbsp. of fresh, minced ginger root

1 cups of cooked brown rice, cooled

4 tsp. of low-sodium soy sauce

1 tsp. rice wine vinegar

How to make it:

1)Coat a large nonstick skillet with cooking spray; heat over medium heat. 2) Add egg and swirl to coat pan; cook egg through.

3) Put cooked egg on a plate and break it up into small pieces with a fork.

4)Off heat, re-coat skillet with cooking spray. Add scallions, pepper, peas and carrots, garlic and ginger to skillet; saut for 5 minutes.

5) Add rice; heat through. Stir in cooked egg, soy sauce and rice wine vinegar.

Sweet Tea

A delicious 2 PPV addition to any meal! This recipe makes 4 servings.

What youll need:

1 cup boiling water

3 tea bags

cup and 2 tbsp white sugar

2 cups cool water

How to make it:

1) Pour boiling water into glass pitcher and add tea bags

2) Cover and allow to steep for 15 minutes

3) Remove tea bags

4) Stir in sugar until dissolved

5) Pour into cool water then refrigerate until cold

Curried Deviled Chicks

Easterisnt Easter without some cute chickadees. Made from our light, delicious Curried Deviled Eggs recipe, these guys are 2 PPV each.

What youll need:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons of fat free-yogurt
  • 2 teaspoons of mild curry powder
  • teaspoon of table salt
  • 1 baby carrot
  • 16 pieces of peppercorn

How to make em:

1) Place eggs in a medium saucepan and fill with enough water to cover them.

2) Bring to boil for 2 minutes.

3) Turn off heat and cover the pan, allowing it to stand covered for 10 minutes on the stove.

4) Place pan in sink and run cold water over eggs until theyre cool.

5) Peel eggs.

6) Cut toward top of the egg, around the middle in a zig zag pattern to create the hatched look.

7) Remove yolks and place them in a small bowl.

8) Mash with yogurt, curry powder and salt.

9) Spoon mixture back into egg whites.

10) Create the chicks faces with peppercorn for eyes and pieces of carrot for noses!
