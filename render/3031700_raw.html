Jackie Kennedy Fashion Tips

Mar 3, 2015 Maria

Its no surprise that Jackie Kennedy had such an impact on the fashion world, she began her fore into fashion when she started as a junior editor at Vogue at the age of 21, Jackie Kennedy fashion style made its mark on the industry but her career at Vogue was short lived. After winning Vogue magazines Prix de Paris essay competition in 1951, Jackie reportedly left the magazine on her first day. Rumored to have left because she was looking for a suitor and would have a hard time finding one in Vogues female dominated environment.

Jackie Kennedy Fashion Tips

This fashion icons classic look can be embodied in timeless pieces and wardrobe staples, weve included some quick tips for fashionistas below:

Oversized sunglasses are a must. They made a big comeback in the last decade but these iconic accsesories are synonymous with Jackie Os style. While adding this accessory to your wardrobe is essential, its also important to match the glasses to the shape of your face.

Sleeveless button-downs can be seen showcased in many of Jackie Kennedys paparazzi shots, especially during the summer and while shes vacationing with her family.

Little Black Dresses are a simple and chic wardrobe staple for any fashionista. Easy to dress up for an evening out and easy to dress down for an afternoon date, Jackie Kennedys simple approach to to building a classic wardrobe included this must-have piece.

Neck Scarves were popular in her heyday and not an easy to wear accessory for most women, without mirroring a flight attendant. However a classic printed scarf can polish off any simple look, the next time youre wearing a classic white T and trousers, pair it with a patterned scarf to add a sense of glam to your look.

Monochrome mastery. Jackie Kennedy was exceptional at pairing monochromatic pieces for an elegant yet chic look

Pillbox hat is a throwback to 1950s fashion. Accompanied by a shift dress or vintage suit, a pillbox hat can make your look more sophisticated

Hair and makeup were always natural and simple. Her understated hairstyle was typically in a bouffant or a pillbox bob. Her makeup was always minimalistic, nude or rose lips and a tint of blush.

Jackie Kennedys more natural approach to makeup, hair and clothing made her aesthetic universally appealing. Oversized sunglasses, little black dresses and neck scarves are a few simple items to complete your Jackie Kennedy fashion inspired ensembles.
