Shrimp Scampi with Linguine

02/28/15 Recipes 13 Chrisy
02/28/15 Recipes 13 Chrisy

Its been another hectic week over here at our house, but in all the good ways: we have company visiting, and although I wish the circumstances were better its still been nice having someone to spend the long weekdays with while The Boyfriend is still at work. Now that Im working from home, its (surprisingly) easy to forget how nice long, thoughtful conversations can be. Usually I make do by making silly small talk with the dogs(way more than I probably should), but nothing beats just sharing thoughts and stories with someone for hours.

Another great perk of having someone in the house with me? I have help eating trying out all the recipes I make! And better yet, they can even tell me if thephotographsI took of said is mouthwatering enough be worthy of the internets. Its a win win if you ask me.

The good news about this recipe (and its photos)? They both got positive reviews. Score!

I have to be careful when it comes to seafood recipes, because Im such pushover when it comes to shrimp you can almost guarantee thatIll eat it and love it and want seconds 5 minutes ago. The Boyfriend doesnt share my enthusiasm for shrimp (or seafood in general), so I have to space out my shrimp fixes with pasta and chicken and steakto help keep the food peace. But with this recipe I got to cheat,since I made it for lunch for the just guest and I.

I felt a little sneaky about it, but in all the good ways: I got to have twice as many helpings as I normally do.

This shrimp scampi recipe is more lemony than it is buttery , but it compliments the shrimp and pasta extremely well. Add in the fact that it only took me 15 minutes to make, I could easily see this being my new guilty pleasure lunchwhenever I need myseafood fix.

ingredients:
  • 8 oz linguine
  • 2 tbsp butter unsalted
  • 1 lb shrimp peeled and deveined
  • 2 tsp garlic minced
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes or more, to taste
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 pinch salt to taste
  • 1 pinch pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp parsley fresh and chopped (or 1 tbsp dried)
  • 1/4 cup parmesan cheese grated
CHANGE QUANTITY? servings CHANGE UNITS?
instructions:
  1. In a large pot, cook linguine in salted water to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. While pasta cooks, melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic, red pepper flakes, and raw shrimp. Stirring occasionally, cook until shrimp are pink and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Pour in white wine and lemon juice, then season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then remove from heat.
  4. Stir in pasta and parsley. Sprinkle top with parmesan cheese. Serve immediately.
