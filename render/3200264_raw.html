Why You Shouldn't Try

I'M saving

you

time

thought by steadyhandsbob
2 min read
  • PERSPECTIVE
Photo by 33170765@N04 / CC BY

Successful people are always telling you to try. Telling you to pursue your dreams. It's an inspiring ideology that encourages you not to stay content in your safe existence, but to make a leap, to follow the passion that would allow you to achieve self-actualization if you just took the chance. Don't take that chance. They are wrong, you shouldn't even try, and here is why.

For everybody who has achieved their dreams, there are tens of thousands of people that don't. Tens of thousands of people who are now in a worse place. These people passed on more realistic opportunities, opportunities that would have put them in a better place. But they're not. They tried and failed.

No matter how good you are at something, there is always an 8 year old Chinese kid who can do it better. There are 7 billion people on this earth, and most of them are dreaming of the same thing you are. Do you really think you can compete with all of them? Chances are half of them are more capable and competent than you are.

You do not have a purpose. You were not hand picked by some divine being to paint the world's most renowned piece of art. You are not destined to be the next Steve Jobs. Chances are you are confusing passion with delusion, and by the time you have explored and exhausted all your "passions," it will be too late and you will be left with nothing.

None of your friends are following theirs, and they are happy. They have found stability, they have found peace. They are buying homes, they are getting dogs, and they are finding happiness, all because they stuck to the path that is proven to work.

That great idea you have has most likely been done before. If it doesn't appear that way, it's because it was a shitty idea and it didn't work. You will be wasting your time trying to accomplish something that has previously been proven to fail.

If you truly want to be happy, save yourself from mental torment and heartache and just put your dreams to rest now. Keep them in your thoughts between midnight and seven, wake up, put on your suit and tie and go to fucking work. I promise you will learn to find happiness in complacency.

If after reading this you have been convinced to forget your dreams, to lay down your wild ambitions, great. I have done my job. Because if some fucking post on the internet can convince you to give up, then somewhere along the lines you would give up anyway, and this is saving you a bunch of time. You can't just follow your dreams, you have to fight for them. You have to kick ass and rip throats along the entire path, and each day only gets more and more difficult. If you stick to it, you will only get stronger.

When it comes down to it, life is a yes or no question. There is no in between. Do you want it? Yes or No.
