Helpful Hints For Healthy Eating On A Budget

How School And Nursery Caterers Are Encouraging Children To Eat A Balanced Diet

Published February 6, 2014 | By dan

School dinners have been in the news a lot over the last couple of years, as the government are encouraging schools to follow healthy eating guidelines. This is to ensure that all children are offered a varied, nutritious meal during their school day. School or nursery caterers will therefore offer meals consisting of high quality meat, poultry or fish, at least 2 portions of fruit or vegetables, plus potatoes, bread or other cereals to all children who have a school meal. Schools are requested not to offer children more than 2 portions of deep-fried food per week and fizzy drinks, crisps, chocolate and sweets should also be avoided.

Even those children who take packed lunches into school are requested to follow strict healthy eating guidelines, to ensure that all children within school have a nutritious and well balanced meal, regardless of whether they have a packed lunch or a school dinner. Most schools will provide clear recommendations for parents regarding what is seen as acceptable for lunchbox contents so fizzy drinks, crisps and sweets are all considered banned.

Many parents struggle to decide whether to send their child to school with a lunchbox or whether to pay for school meals. Buying school dinners does mean that parents can rest assured that their child will be offered a substantial and healthy meal, though for some parents this can be costly. However, the Department for Education has agreed that from September 2014, all children in Reception, Year 1 and Year 2 will be offered a free school meal, which can make life a lot easier for parents.

Nursery caterers work hard to provide a healthy yet tasty menu for school children and will rotate the meal options so that children have different choices of meal each week. Most schools will publish the menus on the school website, which can help parents to find out what their child will have to eat on a particular day, which is handy for parents deciding what to make for the evening meal.
