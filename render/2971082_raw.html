Future Islands Samuel T. Herring is Normcore

share
In Miller by Art Tavana / August 27, 2014
@noisejourno

If Normcore had an ambassador, it would be Samuel T. Herring, the frontman of Baltimore synthpop band Future Islands . On and off the stage, Herring dresses like a department store clerk, or in some cases, a mid-80s jazz pianist. Hes also known for his sexy dance moves, which like his fashion sense (and vocal delivery) are throwback; like a dad revisiting his new wave record collection at a BBQ, loaded on screwdrivers and whatever else possesses someone to move like a Velociraptor.

At FYF Fest this year, Herring came out dressed like a stockbroker on his lunch break. During the Future Islands set, he would use his body to project deep emotion, and before unleashing his unnerving growl, Herrings thick eyebrows would create a look of suffering on his face, as if he was asking the audience for empathy. A few people in the crowd, including myself, thought he sounded like a melodramatic amphibian coming off a bender. But almost everyone else was trying to imitate his patented moves, stuff best suited for a wedding reception rather than a rock festival like FYF. Then again, Herring doesnt carehes normcore, the face of a movement. His GIFs on Tumblr get more shares than Miley Cyrus twerking. And why nothis facial gestures defy the universal laws of how the human forehead should operate.

Hes also a part-time rapper, under the alias Hemlock Ernst, cutting bedroom freestyle sessions that sound like a high school English teacher imitating KRS-One. And he does it dressed like a character from the short-lived 90s series, The Heights.

Then again, thats the effect hes going for. It even got David Letterman to say let dance during the now infamous Future Islands appearance on The Late Show in March, which was an absurd thing to witness. Not bad for the frontman of a three-piece synthpop outfit that creates music thats overly melodramatic, to the point of being operatic, occasionally dancing dangerously close to cringe-worthy.

Future Islands have a cult-like following based mostly on Herrings goofy stage presence; which is either genius, or the result of growing up without a mirror. Its also dynamic, and in many ways, the opposite of everything we consider to be cool, which can raise some eyebrows. Which is also pretty cool.
