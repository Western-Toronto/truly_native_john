The Money Saving-Off

1 year ago
|
Author: Victoria Cawthorne
(0)

Its on. Two of our Make Money Go Further team are taking on money saving challenges. Both are trying different approaches. Both are confident. But who will save the most?

Vickys 5 Savings Challenge

I consider myself to be pretty good at managing my money, but one thing that I havent quite got the hang of yet is how to save it. Making my cash stretch to the end of the month isnt a problem but I have barely any savings, as if I do have any money left over in the last few days of the month, Im always tempted to go out and spend it!

Thats why Im setting myself a money-saving challenge that is rather unconventional, but I think will be easy and fun. Every time I receive a 5 note Im going to save it, with absolutely no exceptions. No, not even if I pay for a cuppa with a 20 note and receive three 5 notes in my change every 5 note is being put away.

This is a challenge that will require discipline, but as 5 is such a small amount to save at a time, I dont think I will even notice the money missing from my weekly budget. In fact, Im hoping that it will discourage me from spontaneous and unnecessary purchases.

My challenge is already underway, and Ill be posting regular updates on my progress. I cant wait to see how much Ill save!

Photo source: Dave Lanovaz

Jamess Money Saving Challenge

I think it is fair to say that I fall into the majority when it comes to saving money I like the idea of it but often set unrealistic targets. So Im trying a different approach, like the one in the image below but saving weekly, rather than monthly (one of the reasons I often fail) and in smaller amounts.

However, I am putting my own unique spin on it. Because putting 50, 51 and 52 away in December feels unrealistic, I am attempting (in theory at least) to be more logical and strategic. I have planned out the 52 weeks and set an amount I will save that week.

So, if I have a lot of plans I might commit to less each week. But every payday week I will put more away. I am trying to manage my own expectations and reducing failure which for me anyway is one of the reasons I so easily end up spending.

It looks like fun and I really like the idea of putting a little away to save a lot. Ill keep you posted on my progress.
