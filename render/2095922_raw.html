The Best Designed Hotels of 2014

This year ushered in plenty of hotel redesigns and openings. Here are 14 standouts

Ace Hotel, Los Angeles
L.A.s hottest destination is the recently opened downtown Ace Hotel, located in the historic 1927 United Artists building. This place has everythingartisan furniture, period details, cartoon sketches, Stumptown coffee, turntables, fashionistas, Mary Pickfords screening room. Oh, and theres a 1,600-seat theater dripping in demented Spanish Gothic ornament inspired by a cathedral in Segovia.

Photo: Spencer Lowell

1 of 15
Cheval Blanc, St. Barts
After successful launches in Courchevel, France, and the Maldives, Cheval Blancthe hotel arm of the LVMH luxury groupset its sights on the Caribbean island of St. Barts for its third resort and spa. Located on the sublime Baie des Flamands beachfront, the 40-room Cheval Blanc St-Barth Isle de France (formerly the Hotel Saint-Barth Isle de France) features a sun-washed palette with tropical accents. London decorator Bee Osborn reimagined the interiors, outfitting the rooms with comfortable modern furniture, French linens, charming illustrations, and sculptural driftwood.

Photo courtesy of Cheval Blanc St-Barth Isle de France

1 of 15
The Peninsula Hotel, Paris
The Peninsula Hotels debuted its first European location in Pariss 16th arrondissement this year. Originally opened as a hotel in 1908, the property hosted numerous legendary guests, from George Gershwin and Igor Stravinsky to Pablo Picasso and Marcel Proust.

Photo courtesy of The Peninsula Hotels

1 of 15
Rancho Mirage, Palm Springs, California
Ritz-Carlton makes a stylish return to Palm Springs, California, with its Rancho Mirage property, perched in the foothills of the Santa Rosa Mountains above the Coachella Valley. Opened as a Ritz-Carlton in 1988 and later rebranded, the hotel recently rejoined the companys portfolio with renovated interiors and two new buildings by SB Architects.

Photo courtesy of The Ritz-Carlton, Rancho Mirage

1 of 15
W Lakeshore, Chicago
At the newly redesigned W Lakeshore, which opened in June, two influences are immediately apparent: Chicagos unique architectural heritage (the Home Insurance Building, dating from 1884, is considered the worlds first skyscraper) and the lake itself. New Yorkbased design firm Meyer Davis handled the $38 million refresh, and the overall aesthetic balances smooth geometric lines with intricate, psychedelic patterns.

Photo courtesy of Meyer Davis

1 of 15
One&Only Hayman Island
For its first property in the land down under, One&Only Resorts has made itself at home on Hayman Island off the coast of Queensland, Australia. The northernmost of the Whitsunday Islands, Hayman comprises more than 700 acres of lush terrain. The resortformerly occupied by another private-island retreatrevealed its sleek new look by Australian firm DBI Design this year.

Photo courtesy of One&Only Resorts

1 of 15
Pikaia Lodge, Galpagos Islands
On the island of Santa Cruz in the Galpagos, the 14-room carbon-neutral Pikaia Lodge abuts a giant-tortoise reserve. The steel, glass, and stone hotel offers a compelling alternative to the standard boat-bound tours of this Ecuadoran ecosystem.

Photo: Manual Tama

1 of 15
Sandibe Okavango Safari Lodge, Botswana
In Botswanas Okavango Delta, AndBeyond has given its Sandibe Okavango Safari Lodge an arresting overhaul. The main building has been reimagined as a swooping shingle-clad stunner, with 12 freestanding suites overlooking the Moremi Game Reserve.

Photo courtesy of AndBeyond

1 of 15
Four Seasons The Westcliff, Johannesburg, South Africa
After a year and a half of extensive renovations, the Four Seasons The Westcliff Johannesburg recently reopened. Set beside the colorful Johannesburg Zoo and overlooking a series of leafy suburbs known as the Parks, the hotel has 117 rooms spread across nine buildings. The hotels makeover, courtesy of RSL Architects, Jacket Interiors, and the designers at Blacksheep, marries the traditional Four Seasons sensibility with Johannesburgs vibrant cultural scene.

Photo courtesy of the Four Seasons

1 of 15
Eau Palm Beach Resort & Spa, Palm Beach, Florida
"It was kismet," interior designer and potter to the stars Jonathan Adler says of the call to reimagine the 309 guest rooms and suites at the Eau Palm Beach Resort & Spa. Adler, who has kept a pied--terre in Palm Beach for over a decade, combined his signature exuberant style with motifs from the areas rich design legacy.

Photo courtesy of Eau Palm Beach Resort & Spa

1 of 15
Tortuga Bay Hotel, Dominican Republic
Fashion icon Oscar de la Rentawho was a partner in the Puntacana Resort & Club, where he kept a homewas called upon at the beginning of the year to redecorate Tortuga Bay Hotel, one of the properties within the 15,000-acre community. Known for his sophisticated style in both fashion and decor, the late designer was a great supporter of his native Dominican Republic. The one-, two-, three-, and four-bedroom suites, which are housed in 13 villas, are luxuriously simple. Patterned fabrics from the Oscar de la Renta line for Lee Jofa add interest to the classic furnishings, which are made of natural materials such as mahogany, wicker, and rattan.

Photo by Puntacana Resort & Club

1 of 15
Ham Yard Hotel, London
For their eighth property in London, Firmdale Hotels owners Tim and Kit Kemp chose to build from the ground up on a previously deserted plot in the citys Soho neighborhood. The duo commissioned architecture firm Woods Bagot to conceive the 91-room Ham Yard Hotel, which surrounds an oak-shaded courtyard garden. In addition to the guest rooms, the complex has indoor and outdoor dining areas, 24 apartments, 13 curated specialty shops, a 190-seat movie theater, a spa, a rooftop terrace, an orangery, and a 1950s bowling alley that was imported from Texas.

Photo courtesy of Firmdale Hotels

1 of 15
Iconic Santorini
At the Iconic Santorini hotel, carved into a cliff on the Greek island, everyday concerns seem to dissolve into the azure Aegean below. The sparkling whitewashed property, near the west-coast village of Imerovigli, is a perfect base for exploring the popular towns of Oia and Fira as well as ancient sites, beaches, and vineyards.

Photo by Kyle Richard St. John

1 of 15
American Trade Hotel, Panama City
Created by the Los Angeles design firm Commune and architect Hildegard Vasquez, the 50-room, five-story American Trade Hotel in Panama City is the latest property from the trendsetting Ace Hotel group. Located in the historic Casco Viejo areawhich Communes Roman Alonso describes as a hybrid of old Havana and New Orleans the 1917 building is appointed with iconic Bertoia chairs and custom-made pieces inspired by vintage Mexican and French furnishings.
