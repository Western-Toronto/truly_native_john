BAG MAN: ADAM BLITZER OF BLUE CLAW CO.

Adam Blitzer the 26-year-old founder of Blue Claw luggage has entrepreneurship in the bag.

Since Adam Blitzer founded his company Blue Claw in 2010, the brand has become synonymous with travel accessories that embody timelss design, functional application, and American heritage crafsmanship.

It all started with the Worton Weekender. After being unable to find a carryall that would suit both his classic style and modern day lifestyle, Blitzer brought a rough sketch of his dream duffel to his grandfather, a veteran of the manufacturing industry, to see what he could do. Less than 24 hours later, the prototype for the company's first bag was in his hands.

"Making our bags in America was never a question. Being new to the business, I wanted to oversee every aspect of production," says the Philadelphia native, who left a career in finance to dive deep into his new design endeavor. "Originally, we made our bags in New Jersey so I could see samples come together piece by piece, select the hardware I wanted, and build the line by picking everything myself. It was a nice added bonus that the company was providing jobs."

Pin

Once he felt he had nailed that first bag's design, Blitzer set up a basic website. Using friends and the press to get the word out, the first 50 bags sold through even more quickly than he expected. It turned out Blitzer had a hit on his hands. "People would say, 'I think the website is broken. I can only find one bag," he says with a laugh. With momentum building, Blitzer pushed to round out his first collection with a garment bag and a dopp kit, and take what would come to be known as Blue Claw Co. to market. "We were quickly seen as a cool group reinventing American design. People were really impressed -- enough so that two big stores took some chances on us. Having their stamp of approval opened a lot of doors."

Since then, the company has moved its tightly-run operation to a factory it purchased in Minneapolis, Minnesota -- where they produce all of their goods in-house without compromise. "We've always wanted the bags to be attainable, and this is how we're able to offer our products at such a value. We took out the middle man."

But turning one ruggedly sophisticated duffle into a full range of travel must-haves takes quintessential American ingenuity and old-fashioned elbow grease. "As an entrepreneur, your to-do list is never done," says Blitzer. "My staff thinks I'm a workaholic, but we're a small and humble team willing to do everything we need to do to get it all done," says Blitzer. "If you love what you're doing, it's not truly work."
