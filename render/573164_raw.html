Its good to share infrastructure for risk management

November 5, 2013 Chris Pickles 0

One of the common themes at recent industry events has been sharing, because financial institutions can no longer each afford to duplicate internally what every other financial institution is having to do. One example of this is the area of risk management and dealing with customers.

How a financial institution checks out a new customer is perhaps not an area that financial regulators would consider as being competitive. Theres a reasonable expectation that a certain level of know-your-client (KYC) expertise and rigour is applied as a lowest-common-denominator across the industry. On that basis, the methodologies, processes and data could be shared more cost-effectively between financial institutions, rather than each of them duplicating all of the processes and costs that are involved. Banks already share data in similar areas, such as around anti-money laundering (AML) and anti-fraud operations.

In todays networked world, sharing doesnt have to mean the same as building a big central utility thats very much a 1970s approach to sharing. Instead, financial institutions can access appropriate shared services across a cloud platform. This not only means that they can share lowest-common-denominator services, but they can also access higher-value services when they want to take their security and risk management to a higher level. This allows any individual bank to differentiate itself where it sees that this benefits both its business and its customers, but without having to do everything itself.

Accessing and sharing risk management services and sensitive customer data in a cloud environment can also bring risks of its own. With so many people thinking that cloud is just a new buzz-word for the public internet, its no wonder that managers in financial institutions get put off by anything related to cloud. But there are private managed cloud platforms too secure ones that are built specifically to meet the sharing needs of financial institutions and that they themselves have been using for over a dozen years now. One of these is the BT Radianz Cloud platform, and through this BT today supports the largest secure networked financial community in the world.

Its time to look beyond the terminology to see the full potential of shared services in the financial sector. Were ready to talk you through the possibilities when you are.
