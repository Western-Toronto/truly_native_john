Sailor Jerry: From Sea Scum to Best Rum

Tyler Stevenson
October 17, 2013

Often when I drink with others, I find myself having in-depth discussions with friends about life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness, but about 10 minutes after that we usually find ourselves discussing more important topics; who really shot first Han or Greedo?, How do Smurfs reproduce with only one female? But one question that always comes up is which rum to imbibe Captain Morgan vs. Admiral Nelson vs. Sailor Jerry.

Sure, Nelson is an Admiral who obviously outranks a mere Captain or lowly Sailor, but Captain Morgan is a pirate and therefore not subject to the hierarchy of the Navy.

Sailor Jerry, or Jer-bear as I like to affectionately refer to it , is the clear winner.

As far as regular consumer rums go, there is only one True king: Sailor Jerry. This rum is the undisputed king of flavor, strength, intensity and is known for just being an all -around badass in 750 ml doses.

Sailor Jerry was named for the legendary tattoo artist, schooner captain, hobo, and all around awesome guy by the name of Norman Keith Collins. Most of those snazzy, nautical -themed ,old -fashionedey looking tattoos you see on todays smartass kids were inspired or directly stolen from tattoos that Jerry brought to the public eye. Collins was an honest to God mans man and total badass. The man piloted -masted sailing ships and played a wicked Sax.

Sailor Jerry is a spiced Dark Rum

Meaning it can be used interchangeably with ,and probably will drastically improve any cocktail calling for, any other dark rum. When I say spiced, I mean Spiced; it has a bite both from the whopping 92 Proof as well as the rich taste. However ,this rum is far more versatile than most other dark rums in that Sailor Jerry can be shot straight like your favorite whiskey. Five shots and youll be standing proud as the captain of your own frigate; a whole bottle and youll be the master and commander of your own imperial flotilla. I personally have drunk an entire bottle of Sailor jerry by myself in less than 15 minutes time and then proceeded to beat 4 roommates at Soul Caliber 3 on Nintendo Game Cube, one after the other.

If youre truly serious about setting off to Sea with this salty dog, be prepared. The Sailor doesnt always leave the dock again without a few parting words. Essentially, you should prepare to wake up feeling like your were shanghaied into a moderate hangover. But for a rum this dark and a proof this high, youd expect it to be much worse. There isnt really much fault to be found with this, the undisputed king of the high seas.

Vital Stats:

Price: $9-$20 for a 750ml bottle, depending on location and applicable taxes.

Location: Higher-end liquor stores, and some grocery stores depending on how shitty your states laws.

Rating: 9/10
