6 Unusual Dining Experiences in Toronto

Andrea Banerjee 2015-04-11
Andrea Banerjee April 11, 2015
0 1.8k 0

We all have that favourite dining spot we default to. Maybe its a cozy wine bar with amazing tapas. Or ahole-in-the-wall diveservingthe best tacos of your life. Or an all-you-can-eat sushi spot that sees you reaching dizzying new heights of tempura consumption.

But whether you consider yourself an adventurous eater or not, its always fun to opt for an experience thats totally novel.

Ever since the recession (paired with a saturated and competitive restaurant scene) challenged chefs to come up with new ways of selling dining experiences, innovative ways of eating out are on the rise. Today, supper clubs, underground dining, and interactive meals are an integral part of the citys culinaryidentity; a way for emerging chefs to debut, and for established chefs to focus on their craft without the distracting crowds or fetters of a conventional menu.

Whether you love the idea of trying a secret dinner, a chefs menu, or a gourmet dive experience, theres no excuse to let your dinners get figuratively stale in a city like this.

Andif you just like to surprise your palate? Well there are lots of great ways to do that too.

Check out some of Torontos fun and out-of-the-box dining experiences and choose your new favourite way to mix up your foodie adventures!

Become a Sushi Purist at Menuless Yasu

Inspired by the documentary Jiro Dreams of Sushi , Yasu is as unique a dining experience as it is an exclusive one. The omakase menu will leave you in capable, if unpredictable hands. Offering around 20 pieces of sushi, each item is painstakingly prepared and served at a tempo thats paced for savouring. The atmosphere is ceremonial and intimate, and the food is always centre stage here. Tune your tastebuds for a hyper-focused meal!

Feast like Moroccan royalty at The Sultans Tent

The wonderfully gaudy French Moroccan restaurant is a whimsical destination for guests who love a theme night (or hummus. I love both). Jewel tones, crushed velvet cushions and tassels form a rich backdrop to the fun and lively environment where belly dancers emerge from strung tapestries as you sit nibbling olives in a romantically draped alcove. The Sultans Tent makes a fundinner date spot, but they also put onan impressive brunch if youre looking to shake up your Sunday morning with someMerguez Sausage & Eggs orBeghrir (Moroccan pancakes).

Eat from the bottom of the food chain at The Atlantic

The nondescript Dundas West restaurant made quite a name for itself when it earned a very favourablereview from Million Dollar Critic, Giles Coren . Known for including somewhat unseemly creatures mollusks and crickets, for example the fare here is menu-less and free offixed prices. Instead, diners are left in experimental chef Nathan Isbergs hands, and are encouraged to pay what makes sense to you based on what you have had. And for patrons with only have a couple bucks in their pocket? They need only tell Nathan and hell whip something up thats filling and fast at the bar counter.

Have the most laid-back meal of your life at 3030 Dundas West

With a new craft brew each time you visit, pinball machines and board games, this place is set up for a relaxing night in the Junction. While their craft beer menu draws crowds (and represents around 14 different breweries), their small plates are not to be overlooked. Based on the premise that delicious food should be affordable, these dishes are totally shareable but youll want to hog them.

Try a drop-in dinner at The Depanneur

The Depanneur is a one-of-a-kind dining space and shop, hosting tons of events that win favour among foodies like supper clubs and cooking classes. But its their drop-in dinners that really give The Depanneur its local fan base. Prepared by a rotating roster of chefs, the dinners are 100% reservations-free and served to diners who can sit sprawled across the space, community-style. Diners are also welcome to bring containers for take-out, and wine for pairing (and added merriment). Check out full details about this awesome event here .

Attempt to land an invite to Charlies Burgers Supper club

Known as one of the best and most mysterious dining experiences in the city, Charlies Burgers accepts only a small (and lucky) group of diners for its elusive invitation-only underground suppers. Once accepted into the secret circle, attendees are given scavenger hunt clues to find out where and how their meal will be hosted.

Previous locations have ranged from penthouses to cellars, and have featured a number of headliner chefs. If youre looking for details, you wont find them, but you can toss your hat in the ring by visiting the sort-of-officialwebsite.

Happy adventures, Hogtownfoodies!
