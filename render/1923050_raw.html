9 Celebrities Who Perfected Their Smiles With Cosmetic Dentistry

Posted on April 4, 2014 by Dr. Edward Gelfand

Not everyone has perfect teeth from the start including celebrities! For different reasons people may need cosmetic dentistry. From dental veneers to total smile makeovers , these celebrities have needed a good dentist with a plan to get their A-List smiles ready for the red carpet.

Here are 9 Celebrity Smiles That Have Been Fixed Or Copied With Cosmetic Dentistry!

BRAD PITT

Brad Pitt agreed to go to the dentist and have hisown teeth chippedto playFight ClubsTyler Durden.

HALLE BERRY

Many fans admire Berrys infectious smile. Apparently, her smile is even inspiring many fans to get press-on veneers that are identical to Halle Barrys grin.

OLSEN TWINS

Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen had to wear fake teeth during the later years of Full House because their smiles began to look different.

GEORGE WASHINGTON

George Washington soaked his dentures in port overnight to improve their taste. He had many sets; some were made of elephant tusks, lead, cow teeth, hippo teeth, human teeth and possibly a walrus tusk.

SHERYL CROW

Sheryl Crows two front teeth are fake. She had them knocked out when she tripped on the stage early on in her career.

BEN AFFLECK

MTV reported that Michael Bay, Armageddon director,wanted Ben Affleck to star in the film desperately, that he paid to have the Bens tiny teeth capped.

MONTGOMERY CLIFT

When actor Montgomery Clift was in a car accident, Elizabeth Taylor was one of the first to arrive at the scene. Noticing that he was choking, Liz performed a finger sweep in Clifts mouth only to find his two front teeth were the obstruction! Because she saved his life, Clift had the teeth mounted as a gift for Taylor.

HARRISON FORD

Early in his career Ford fell on a gun during a stunt for a TV appearance. Afterward, a dentist had to pull two of his teeth.

JOHN LENNON

John Lennon had a rotten molar removed in the 1960s. The tooth was then given as a gift to his housekeeper, whose family kept it.. In 2011 a Canadian dentist purchased it in an auction for more than $31,000.
