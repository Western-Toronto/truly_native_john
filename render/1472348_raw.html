How to become the MacGuyver of Website Speed

Maybe you have seen several other posts on website speed and theres a reason for that. It does matter.

As your journey goes on and you dive deeper into the dark world of SEO, programming and website optimization you get a feeling what things are important and which ones arent. I can asure you, web speed IS imprtant.

This is the guide for all people running slow WordPress websites, but lacking the technical ability or even motivation to do all that boring onpage speed optimization. Ill show you the MacGuyver way to use plugins, quick hacks and Bubblegum only to get your website up and running like a rocket!

Why does website load time matter?

No matter where you look at, people are scratching their heads and talk about this topic. I found articles on Copyblogger , interesting thoughts from SEO guru Matthew Woodwards and also various research mentions about the huge impact on conversion on big retail sites like Amazon.

+ 100 ms 1% sales @Amazon

+ 500 ms 20% traffic @Google

Source: Make Data Useful

You get the picture? Maybe those numbers arent that important for your small blog or retail site, but it can have a pretty significant impact on your rankings too. Lets listen what Matt Cutts has to say about that topic:

Okay, enough of that. Lets come to the meaty part and have a look at what you could do to speed up your WordPress website and shoot up in rankings and conversions.

What you will learn about speeding up WordPresssites

There are certain steps you can perform to ramp up a notch and get your website to load faster. I will explain everything to you step by step.

  • Benchmark your site and check how fast/slow it is right now
  • Get on quality hosting& set up a DNS
  • Install a good caching plugin and configure it
  • Optimize your Images
  • Minify Javascript and CSS files
  • Use Google libraries
  • Find time robber and fix them

Okay, now you know what were going to do. Read on to find out how to optimize everything for blazing fast appearance on the web!

What to work with? Benchmark your site

First of all you need to know how your WordPress site is performing right now. This means youll have to measure load time somehow.

I recommend several free tools to use. This will give you an overview and a broader range of measured times to get a better picture. These tools also allow you to see why your website slows down and how to fix it.

#1 Google Page Speed Insights| Use it

A popular free tool provided by big ol Google itself. It allows you to check if your website is mobile compliant and also if there are any page speed issues.

You only get shown a percentage though and no actual loading time. It even lists the issues on your website and gives hints how to solve specific problems.

It is definetly possible to improve this score with some changes on your blog, but I was only able to achieve 85/100 when I tried last time. This is due to some external scripts I run on my site like LeadPages , social network buttons or even Google Analytics.

Page Speed Insights doesnt recognize that I am not able to minify these files, because I have no control over them.

I highly recommend you check this tool out and have a look what you can do to improve the numbers. If youre stuck just ask me or hire an expert to do it for you.

#2 WebPageTest.org| Use it

Another free resource I love and recommend. It allows you to run a test for your blog and get back a time how your website did in case of page load time, but also a bunch of analytics and insights what to improve.

It gives you:

  • A summary

The summarygives you everything you need to know at a glance. Not that detailed, but if there are bigger issues yoUll probably recognize it.

  • Details

This shows you a complete waterfall view of the site performance and helps to find huge images or scripts.

  • Performance review

On that tab theres a table to provide you with several easy to recognize action items. They are marked by yellow exclamation marks or red crosses.

  • Content breakdown

Last but not least this allows you to check where your top priority is. A visual content breakdown to see if theres loads of CSS, images, HTML or anything else.

Id call this a more advanced tool, but Im sure youll figure it out. Definetly helpful to find hidden issues to fix further down the road.

#3 GTMetrix| Use it

This time its a tool powered by Yahoos YSlow feature to check your performance across all search engines. It will show you a colored list of all loaded files and sort them from red over yellow to green.

Read the tips provided for each and try to fix it where possible. Also shown is a percentage grade how well your site did and actual metrics for the load time and page size.

Simple to use, not much more to explain here so check it out.

#4 Pingdom| Use it

Everybodys darling: Pingdom. This is the tool I found mentioned all across the web and everybody seems to know about it.

Pingdom allows you to do a one-off measure of your site and shows similar stuff to the WebPageTest.org tool (just in a nicer interface) or you can register for free and it will monitor your blog every one minute and notify you the momentissues appear.

I like it, because you can subscribe to it and its very fast. it also allows you to sort data by load time to easily identify whats slowing down your website.

#5P3 (Plugin Performance Profiler)

It is available for free from the WordPress plugin repository. Its one of those tools youll want to bookmark for future reference. Try it out on your site today and find out how easy it is to track down the plugins that are slowing your site down.

After running a scan check if you can delete the slow plugins and add some code or a faster plugin. Maybe you can even delete it and just go on with your blogging life

Now you should have a good overview about your websites performance. note down or print out the results for later. You need it to compare if your results lead to better values.

Where to host and WTF is a CDN?

Were going to start the site optimization right now. Assuming you set up your WordPress website in a self hosted version you already have a hosting provider.

Thats great. BUT it seems to be the wrong one, otherwise you wouldnt be here.

If you currently host your site with a cheap provider like Hostgator or BlueHost theres nothing wrong with that, but they are known to be slow. To speed up your website its important to be on a dedicated hosting server or use special WordPress hosting.

Due to the experiences Matthew Woodward had and also because I am a big fan of using ANY plugin I want to, I do not recommend WP Engine for hosting but use another company instead.

My host of choice is Traffic Planet Hosting which is a fantastic service for a great price and also comes with really fast support. I had to use them several times and they always helped or at least replied in under an hour!

Several things they helped me with include:

  • Enable WP Multisite for one of my blogs
  • Fixing issues I caused myself using a broken plugin
  • Adding domains to my account
  • other minor issues

Yes, I am an affiliate for their hosting but I really like it and use it myself. I had accounts on several SEO hosting platforms and also Hostgator and Bluehost, but this is the premium class.

In case you decide to check them out let me know what you think. Since I use their service my time for the first byte has increased a lot and most of the day my site loads under two or even one second.

Almost as important as the question of your WordPress hosting is it to set up a CDN to serve your static files. There are two options:

  • Cloudflare (free or paid, I currently use free)
  • MaxCDN (paid only, it seems to be even faster)

Of course there are other options, but these are the most popular ones. I use a combination of Cloudflare in the free version with W3 Cache plugin. Many other bloggers swear on MaxCDN, but I cant tell for sure if they offer a better experience.

Im happy with cloudflare so far, because it loads my images and other static resources always on a server that is near you to deliverstufffaster. Read on to learn the setup necessary for this.

Optimize your images a lot!

This is one of my favourite topics when it comes to WordPress and I could talk all day long about image optimization. Today I will cover speed optimization only and well keep all that SEO stuff like Alt tags and so on for another post.

How can you optimize your images to load faster than the average Joe? Its easier than you might think.

Lets brake it down into several steps for easy following. First of all get a picture where your images are located across your site. I guess you have featured images, in-post images, maybe video thumbnails, category specific images, advertising banners and a thousand other locations.

Every on of these locations demands another size (in most themes and sites). So a post image will have another size once its loaded as featured image or related post thumbnail. This causes the production and storage of many different images in your WordPress database.

Also many times webmasters use images that are bigger than needed. For example they take a 1400px x 800px Image and use it as a 800px x 300 px post header, only rescaled by WordPress itself. NOT GOOD.

Here are some things I recommend to do to fasten your site by reducing image load time:

#1 Webresizer.com| Use it

This is a free tool I use to scale my images to the right size. It allows you to upload any image, adjust the size and re-download it after optimizing. The service will often reduce your image size by half! I t also compresses the image, but its not very good at doing so.

#2 Kraken.io| Use it

Forget everything you ever heard about Smush.it and use this instead. Its a tool thats quite similar to the one I mentioned above, but the free version only compresses and doesnt allow resizing of images. Its by far the best tool for that and will reduce image size again.

The paid version allows to upload even bigger images, resizing and a WordPress plugin to use with their API. If you can afford it I recommend it highly.

#3Clean Up Images Plugin| Use it

After uploading images you want to delete all unused versions and thats what this plugin does. It scans your website for unused images, lists them and let you decide which ones to delete.

I really love it and it has saved me tons of data space since installing and never deleted one wrong image ever. By the way it also helped me make my related post thumbnails look better (I use YARPP and some CSS), because after running clean up images only the thumbnails with right sizes are left.

#4 BJ Lazy LoadPlugin| Use it

This is the best lazy load plugin I know of and it didnt produce any errors in my site despite me running 29 (!) different plugins. Its free so definetly have a look into this one it makes a big difference.

Minfy& Optimize Javascript and CSS

Always keep your code overhead as low as possible. Plugins and blown up themes can cause a real headache when it comes to page speed, but also tracking like Google Analytics or social buttons.

Here are some plugins I recommend in addition to W3 Total Cache to fix this:

  • Autoptimize to minify CSS/JS/HTML (disable minify in W3 this is better)
  • Google Webfont Optimizer (works fine for me despite the one bad review)
  • Speed Booster Options (adds asynchronously loading CSS and other nice things)
  • Use Google Libraries (Only necessary if you dont use Speed Booster)

For some this might seem overkill and some of the plugins add duplicate functions, but I am no techie and this definetly speeded up my site a lot. Do not forget to backup before playing around with these plugins!

Eliminate Slow Files

You should definetly be on track now and already have achieved a pretty decent website speed, but some things might still be in your way. Just go ahead and use the benchmark tools I mentioned above again.

Have a look at the waterfall views and sort the lists by load time. GTMetrics also is able to show you a list of big images that need optimization use it.

Once you do that keep an eye on the file names and search for plugins or scripts that might cause errors and see if you can fix it. If you need help with that I can recommend QuickFalcon.com (seems to be down right now, check back later if its there).
