Building a Better Relationship with Customers Through Mobile

In Partnership With:

We sat down with Lara Crystal from Minibar Delivery to discuss how mobile devices and single-purpose apps are redefining the way we shop

PSFK Labs
  • 25 february 2015
  • Partner Content

Be sure to check back often for more great articles here and on Braintrees site , and dont forget to download the full Mobile Commerce Playbook on our Slideshare page .

Lara Crystal is the co-founder of Minibar Delivery , a New York City-based startup centered on the delivery of wine and spirits. Through partnerships with local stores, Minibar promises on-demand delivery of alcohol orders to Manhattan and Brooklyn in under an hour. The service automatically connects shoppers with the nearest participating store to create a seamless ordering experience.

We sat down with Crystal to discuss why good design is essential to the mobile experience and how mobile is the key to creating personalized experiences for your customers.

PSFK : What are some of the larger themes you have been paying attention to in the mobile commerce space over the past year?

Lara : A singlepurpose app is definitely something we believe in. People want one app on their screen that does one thing really well particularly with the reduced screen real estate on a mobile device.

Doing one thing well is important, and that definitely was a key insight even before we started Minibar. As we continue to develop, we think about how that experience affects what we build on the app versus what we build on the web.

Our mobile consumer tends to be in an ondemand mindset and wants everything right now, whereas our web consumer tends to plan ahead. For example, we generally see web users shopping for larger orders, so weve built web-specific tools to meet those needs. Our party-planning tool is available on the web, but isnt offered within the app because its not core to the experience of what our app users are doing.

The other thing weve seen is just how important design and development is on mobile. People expect an app to look good, feel good, and work well. Because of this, there are more and more apps that are doing a great job with design, which raises the overall standard.

PSFK : What is the unique benefit of interacting with consumers over mobile as opposed to bricks and mortar?

Lara : In relation to bricks and mortar, a big point is that youre not limited by physical space and its inherent restrictions. You can shop when the store isnt open, and do so from anywhere in the world. This provides retailers with an enormous opportunity in terms of selling, reaching and communicating with consumers.

Another benefit is the realtime data that mobile provides, in terms of how much we know about the consumer, and how much more we will be able to know as people continue using apps and other interfaces on their mobile phones. As a marketer, that creates a huge opportunity.

PSFK : As mobile fluency is on the rise, how do you think it will change the relationship between buyers and sellers?

Lara : That relationship is very important and needs to be nurtured very carefully. Your relationship with the appand therefore those brandsis also crucial. Merchants have to be careful about how theyre communicating with those consumers and how much of what theyre giving is being used to relate back to them.

PSFK : What do you think are some of the challenges that retailers and merchants will begin to face as they adopt mobile as their primary retail channel?

Lara : One challenge is security and how you use a customers data. Different people have different standards. Some people are more careful about sharing their information, and headlining stories about data breaches at big brands (think Target) are only serving to get consumers more scared about where their data is going.

I expect there will be more of these stories before companies step up to really fix the problem. Fixing these security issues and establishing consumer trust is something that payment providers still have a long way to go on.

PSFK : Do you have any predictions that you can share about what we can look forward to in the future of mobile commerce?

Lara : At a baseline, retailers will begin to provide more relevant content within every appdepending on how much they know about their customerand serving all of that up at the right time.

Knowing where a person is, knowing what apps theyve used, all of those things could be components that are given to brands or companies or marketersallowing them to target consumers at various times and at various places, based on when they shop, where they are now, what theyre watching and what other media theyre consuming. I think this kind of relevant, targeted messaging will be huge going forward.
