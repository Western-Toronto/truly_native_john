We currently have an HP Mini Netbook that is showing it's age.

We use it as a "Loaner" Laptop to staff who go out to the field that only have desktop's at the office. We have found that a lot of staff like to carry it on trips because it is lightweight and small.

It is getting old howerver, and I would like to replace it with something along the same cost, and specs. (Maybe not an Atom/1GB RAM, but an i3/4GB RAM would be nice.) It doesn't need to be very powerful, but it does have to be able to accept a corporate Windows 7 Image via PXE boot.

We only paid about US500-600 for it, and it seems like the market is full of ultrabooks that run US700 or over.

Recommendations?

1st Post

Justin,

A lot of customers of mine moved to tablets with attached keyboards for two reasons:

  1. Tablets from Samsung, Asus and even Acer now have so much processing power that the tablet market replaced netbooks as soon as keyboards (like Targus magnetic keyboards) were widespread.
  2. On top of having access now to tablet-based apps for most professional software (Office, Citrix, and even cloud-served apps), you only add to the capabilities of the unit by taking advantage of the touch capabilities for presentations in the field.

I guarantee I could find a tablet less than $400 that would not need to be Intel-based and still run circles around a netbook. Depending on what you are running application-wise, if you are looking at a Windows tablet, like a Slate Pro 2, you are indeed looking at a bit more expensive, but it may be an investment that can get returns in productivity and field effectiveness, especially for field sales.

Have you looked into virtualizing the image and containerizing it on a tablet? That is another option, including using Citrix. I actually just sat through a demo from HP on a Citrix VDI of a windows desktop on an Android-based tablet. That could reduce the cost of your hardware and simplify the imaging requirements, though there might be some cost in the VDI option you use if not comfortable with something open source (all disclosure, I have not sourced open source VDI for Android, so I don't even know if there is such an animal yet).

So many more possibilities these days. Definitely consider feasibility, and work with a vendor that can give you the research time. If you need any specific help, let me know.

joeryder Mar 13, 2014 at 4:56 UTC

A caution on the Inspiron line: that line of Dell products are not business class. Inspirons are consumer-grade, which means the support is less, the quality is less (Dell sources random parts that go end of life quickly) and you get better performance out of a business-class line, like Latitude. I have an auto dealership group that hates their Inspirons because they don't last. However, they also are used a lot, and have special setups that are hard to deal with anyway. It would really depend on what applications you are using and how the field staff will be taking it where it needs to go.

One other concern is durability. Latitudes are more durable and dust-resistent. At least this is the case looking at Dell options. Other companies have other levels of durability.
