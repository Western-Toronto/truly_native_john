Easy Crock Pot Taco Soup

5/24/2015

12 Comments

Today is a super busy day. I will not have time to make dinner later. So that's where my trusty slow cooker comes in! Just a few simple ingredients later and I have a meal in the making while I am busy doing other things! I share with you meals as I go along in my family life and this was one that is one of our go to family meals for years when in a hurry. I just have to share!
Here is what you will need for a large crock pot: ( we make enough to feed a family of 4 and have left overs)
  • 1 can of Hormel Chicken Breast
  • 2-3 cans of black beans (rinsed)
  • 1 can of chili beans
  • 1 can of corn
  • 1 packet of taco seasoning
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions if you like ( my kids do not like onions)
  • Some fresh or packaged chives & cilantro if you have it , it tastes great without it too
  • I box of chicken stock
  • 3 cups of water
  • Stir and cook on high or low depending on the time you need to have it by

It is so super easy to make this. I love the fact I can use Hormel canned chicken breast in a jiffy and be on my way rather than to wait to cook the chicken. I do not have time some days for that! You can even add some Spanish rice to it if you want as well to the soup. We top with sour cream, Mexican blended cheese & serve with corn chips.

Spams Benedict

5/19/2015

10 Comments

Ok my husband and I was wanting to create something amazing with the Spam Jalapeno we had. He loves jalapeno's! So we created Spam's Benedict! It had an interesting kick to it that was an awesome change from your typical Eggs Benedict!
Who says Spam can't be elegant!
The how to:
I will list them in order as you should do it and build your creation

  1. Toast your English muffins
  2. AddSpinach first. You can wilt if you like in your pan just a bit.
  3. Slice your Jalapeno Spam or any Spam to your desired thickness & grill in the pan slightly
  4. Add sliced tomato
  5. Next fry your egg over medium
  6. Make a prepacked Hollandaise sauce to put on top should only take a minute
  7. Garnish with dicedparsley & or cubed tomato
Total cook time ten minutes
This fast meal is simple and elegant. The Jalapeno Spam gives it just the right kick of extra flavor. But of course you could use any Spam!

Come Peek Into The Historic Crabtree Jones House

5/8/2015

10 Comments

Last weekend me & my son went for a drive. We love to sometimes drive and explore the city we live in that we moved to over a year ago. I love randomadventure. We came to a corner thathad anextremelyoldbuilding. They had an open house. We said let's go see! This house is the Crabtree Jones House that used to stand on the lot you see below where the new apartment complex is! Preservation North Carolina saved the house from it's doom of being demolished and moved it only feet away on it's own large lot.
The house is believed the be the oldest remaining residential house left in Raleigh. It is on the market for sale! You can purchase it and renovate it. This house is around 200 years old & is a listed historical site. This Federal style house wasbuiltin 1809 and started off as a one story but transformedover the years.I am so happy I got to go take a peek inside at how it looks now from the years of history before it is bought and renovated into a residentialresidence. So come along with me to peek at the past before it becomes modernized! Forgive thequalityof my photos but all Ihad on hand on this random trip was my cell phone camera!
This flooring! I love it.
Yes there was a fireplace in the hallway!
I am in love with all the old fireplaces in this house!
I love all the old door knobs in this house
The Crabtree Jones house was featured on the cover of Progressive Farmer Magazine back in it's heyday in November of 1934
It was funny seeing hangers left in an old closet from former tenants from God only knows how long ago. The 60's? 70's? 80's?
Old creepy attic
The memories these walls must hold. Amazing to think about it all.
This is the first room you see when you walk in. The mantel art has not yet been dated but they guess it is close to 200 years old.
This must of been the first electric lighting added in the early 1900's. This light is the one as you enter the first greeting room of the house.
There was so much more to this house than I have showed in my pictures. I wanted to just give a glimpse & somewhat documentthis historical home in it's current state before it is renovated. I think the years & stories this old house could tell would be awesome. I think of all the past 200 years of people that lived in this house and how each family saw their own history in the making. If only houses could talk! Thank you for coming along with me to peek into the past.
