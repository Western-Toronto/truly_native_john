How Mobile is Changing the Way We Buy

In Partnership With:
Partner Content
  • Mail
  • Tweet

We sat down with Mike McEwan of Jane.com to discuss how mobile services are changing the products we buy and the frequency with which we buy them

PSFK Labs
  • 12 march 2015
  • Partner Content

Mike McEwan is the founder and CEO of Jane.com , an online and mobile retailer that offers special access to daily deals on boutique clothing. The companys success is founded on building great vendor relationships and in paying its vendors more than any other daily deals site.

We sat down with Mike to discuss how mobile apps are changing our buying behaviors and why mobile buying is quickly overtaking web sales.

PSFK : What are some of the larger shifts that youve been paying attention to in the mobile commerce space during the past year?

Mike : Weve seen a major shift from desktop to mobile. At the beginning of 2014, I realized we were missing out on a huge opportunityso we decided to go fullforce into building an iPhone app.

When we released it in July, we sent out emails to our existing customer base, and started to see about five percent of overall sales coming from our app. By the end of 2014 we were seeing almost 32 percent of all sales coming from it. Its a more native and simpler process that feels much smoother than desktop.

Weve seen a huge shift in that direction, and now were building an Android app that will be done soon. Were shifting a lot of our efforts toward mobiledesigning for mobile first and for web second.

PSFK : What would you say is part of the unique benefit of having this interaction with your customers over a mobile device as opposed to on your website?

Mike : Nobody carries their laptop around with them, but everybody has their phone with them, always. People feel naked without their phones now. You also have push notifications that are more personalized and less intrusive.

People are used to getting a bunch of microcommunications, whereas emails are associated with something that they have to do. These microcommunications through push notifications are a new and very effective way to market to customers.

PSFK : Is our relationship with mobile devices changing how merchants speak to customers?

Mike : Definitely. We have to adapt to our consumers and the way that they live their lives. When we go to bed, we check our phones. When we wake up, its the first thing we do. Taking advantage of those times when you know consumers are going to be on their phones is crucial. Something thats really important is personalizing notifications so they arent generic. If youre personalizing notifications to overlap with consumer interest, they will be more likely to engage.

I think well see that average order values will start to shrink as checkouts become more frictionless and simplified and people buy multiple times a day. So if we send a push notification around a limited-time offer and make it really simple for shoppers to complete a transactioneverythings all set up and they just push a button or two to buytheyll grab the deal before its gone.

PSFK : What would you say are some of the challenges associated with the widespread adoption of mobile?

Mike : I think one of the largest challenges is simply generational. To the younger generation, paying for things on our phones will be normal, just as something like TV was completely normalized when I was growing up. They live in an app-centric world and are more intuitive about how they approach the platform.

PSFK : What should merchants keep in mind as theyre designing mobile experiences for their consumers?

Mike : I think its key to prioritize whats most important for customers up front, and show that in a very simplified way. Weve done testing on our site to pull data in from our deals. Simpler always seems to be better.

With the checkout, its about having the most unobtrusive and super-simple design. Save their information, save their credit card, so when they get to checkout its a matter of just pushing a button.

PSFK : Do you have any predictions about what consumers can look forward to in the future of mobile commerce?

Mike : People are dumping a lot of effort and money into predictive modelingso helping consumers find what they want faster and making more personalized suggestions to them is going to be important.

People who bought this also boughtthat kind of thinking doesnt really take into account any user behavior, the things theyre looking at and how long they looked at them. Taking all these little data points into account, and personalizing suggestions to make e-commerce smarter, will be key.

The other prediction is that the checkout process will become more simplified and frictionless. As a result, were going to see more in-app purchases.

Thanks, Mike!
