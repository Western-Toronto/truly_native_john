Teen Internet Safety Guide

Introduction to Teen Internet Safety

Just a few years ago, the Internet was connected to stationary desktops, which made monitoring kids and teens activity on the web relatively easy. But now, teens have access to always-on connections that are tucked into their back pockets and move with them wherever they go. Keeping a watchful eye on your teens Internet use is no longer possible.

The proliferation of home computers and new technologies, such as smartphones and tablets, are opening up even more opportunities for your teen to use the Internet. Consider this: The U.S. Department of Health and Humans Services reports that in 2013, 93 percent of teens had access to a computer, 78 percent of teens had their own cell phones (46 percent of which were smartphones) and 46 percent of 16 and 17 years olds owned a tablet.

As a parent, you undoubtedly want to support your teens sense of independence and his growing want of privacy, but its no secret that the Internet can be a dangerous place and protecting your teen is top priority. The quandary leaves many parents unsure about the best approach to teen Internet safety, and thats why SatelliteTV.com created this insightful Teen Internet Safety Guide.

Read through the guide and we will provide you with shocking statistics that make it clear you need to monitor your teens online activity. Youll be showered with tips, techniques and advice about how to protect your teen from a whole host of dangers, including fraud and identity theft. This guide will help you know how to teach your teen safe social media behavior and help him or her avoid cyberbullying. Plus, youll discover the best ways to safeguard your teen from the threats posed by email, instant messenger, and chat rooms.

When youre done reading this Teen Internet Safety Guide, we hope youll share it with other parents and educators. Working together, we can help the teens in our homes, schools and throughout our community enjoy the Internet safely.
