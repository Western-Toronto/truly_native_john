A Photographer in Los Angeles was working with Jason Derulo and What Happened Next Was Devastating!

Tweet
Top 5 Cloud Storage Services
Lydia Boyels has been a photographer here in Los Angeles for over 10 years now and she has gained quite a reputation for photographing hollywood stars and working directly with clothing brands to caputre images that really resonate with their buyers. Her hip and edgy style makes her a top choice photographer to work with for pretty much any occasion. But recently Lydia ran into a problem that literally changed her life in an instant.

Most recently Lydia was working on the set of Jason Derulo's music video for "want you to want me" and she was doing her typical things, mingling with the members of the set, taking candid photographs, working directly with Jason to get some great still shots for promotions, magazines etc. Overall Lydia had taken over 1500 pictures for that days shoot. She has always relied on hard drives to store all of her work and at the end of the day she decided to buy an new hard drive to house all the pictures from the days shoot. After purchasing a 1TB hard drive for around $300 Lydia decided that she was going to upload all the pictures from her last 5 projects, after all, why not, she had a brand new hard drive with plenty of space. "This is pretty typical of the industry", She says, "We all store our pictures and video on hard drives, we practically have stacks of them laying around. In fact we have stacks on stacks and we connect them all together and call them stacks." After getting home Lydia got to work uploading everything, a process that took hours to complete.

After the pictures and video from her last 5 projects we're all completely uploaded and deleted off her computer and camera Lydia decided to call it a night and went to bed. The next morning she had to wake up for a 4AM call time and be ready with all of her footage and videos as the video shoot was in the final stages and everyone needed to have all the assets readily available. Lydia grabbed her hard drive and was off to work. During the day Lydia shot many more pictures and gathered some excellent content and she had backed everything up to her new HD. Finally the end of the day came around and everyone was either turning in their footage or reviewing it with the team to see what would be submitted. But there was a problem...

Lydia's hard drive wasn't turing on. It looked for a moment as if it were a power problem so she found a new outlet to plug her HD into and still nothing. "The director of the shoo t was sitting right there with me and watching me not able to access the pictures, at first I brushed it off like it was no big deal, I think I said hold o n, I'll have it in one second, although inside my heart was sinking! I was terrified I would not be able to reboot the HD." After a few attempts and really testing the directors patience and also the executive producer and not to mention the rest of the crew, Lydia finally came to the conclusion that the hard drive was busted! "There was nothing I could do to turn on the HD. I even had some IT guys look at it and that's when we knew it was fried."
And just like that Lydia had lost two days of work on one of the most important projects she had worked on, not only that but she had backed up her last 5 projects on the same HD. "The worst part was that the other 5 projects I had backed up we're not yet delivered to the clients, so....basically I lost 3 months of work in the blink of an eye. I literally wanted to die, I think I cried for 3 hours straight."Lydia went back the store where she got the HD and they told her that sometimes these things happen, due tot he circumstances they fully refunded her money but they were never able to recover all her files ever again.

Lydia had to call all of her clients and tell them the bad news, she refunded 90% of the money she had made over the last 3 months, which really put her in bad shape financially. She lost 3 of her best clients because they had wasted thousands of dollars with her and also they had to redo all of their projects, they weren't happy! And unfortunately she has become known in a small circle of people as that photographer who didn't take extra precaution and lost tons of work for her clients.

"Looking back I guess I could have been more careful, but yo never know with these types of things. It's really difficult to know that I let down my clients, the team I was working with and especially to know that like, this could have all been prevented. I guess it's the standard for our industry to use hard drives but I will never make that mistake again. It's really time for us to shift all of our information storage to online backup or cloud storage so these things just don't happen."

Since the incident Lydia has found it hard to generate new clients based on referral, as you can imagine but...she has learned a huge lesson and she has developed new and safer techniques for storing her work. For example, Lydia is now able to instantly share all of her work with clients through the cloud and because she uses cloud storage she can give access to anyone at anytime. Her new clients, and some old ones are much happier with the new system as they can literally see files uploaded in real time and choose to download and save anything they want. "If anything this has probably been good for business, with my new cloud storage system there is more transparency with my clients and I don't have to lug around an HD or constantly send files via email to the clients, they have immediate access to the cloud all the time. "
