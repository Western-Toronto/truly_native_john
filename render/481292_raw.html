Two Peas in a Pod: The Star-Spangled Banner & Professional Baseball

The Star-Spangled Banner, our nations anthem, is proudly sung before each MLB baseball game some performances more memorable than others of course. However, when looking at all of the performances throughout baseball history, it is easy to forget that our national anthem was written 200 years ago! The history of the Star-Spangled Banner in baseball is set apart from other professional sports which may seem fitting since baseball is considered Americas pastime. What better song to represent a sport so rich in tradition than our own national anthem? Heres a quick look at the history and fun facts about the Star-Spangled Banner and its link to the Baseball world:

Theres a First Time for Everything

Although it was not passed as the United States official national anthem until 1931, the Star-Spangled Banner made its professional baseball debut over 100 years ago, in 1862, at Union Grounds in Brooklyn, New York. Although, the tradition of the anthem playing at a professional baseball game did not pick up momentum until 1918, when it was played at Game 1 of the World Series.

During the 7 th inning stretch, with the Cubs facing the Red Sox at Wrigley field, the military band took the field as usual to play while the fans were standing and stretching it out. They opened up with the Star-Spangled Banner, a fight-song and tribute to those fighting in World War I and the four lives lost a day before, when a bomb hit the Chicago Federal Building. However, it was not the playing of this song that was the story of the day, it was what occurred after the song started.

Fred Thomas, who played 3 rd base for the Sox, was on furlough from the Navy while playing in the World Series. When the song started, Thomas turned toward the flag and held a military salute as the song played. After seeing the gesture, others on the field and eventually the stands joined in, giving the flag a civilian salute with their hands over their hearts. But it wasnt enough for the onlookers to stand and pay their respects, as the song continued, the fans began to sing along, which gave some sound and life to a fairly quiet game to that point.

The actions of the players and fans that day created such a splash in the papers and stadium that the anthem was played at each game of that years World Series. After the series closed and the Sox were dubbed World Series champions, the playing of the Star-Spangled Banner continued the following season specifically during holiday and championship games during the season. It was not until 1942, 11 years after the song was dubbed the national anthem, that the Star-Spangled Banner made a regular appearance before every baseball game.

The Beginning of a Tradition

In 42, World War II raged on as some players were deployed for service. As a tribute to the players and civilians fighting the good fight, the national anthem was slated to play prior to every baseball game. Playing the national anthem at baseball games would remain a tradition for the following seasons in not just baseball, but also picked up by the NFL, NHL and NBA.

Since the tradition started in 1942, the national anthem has been played over 121,000 times including the most recent World Series, which went seven games between the Royals and Giants before the Giants were crowned as the 2014 champions. Not to mention the 2011 and 2002 Series, which also went a full seven games, with the anthem sung prior to each game.

So as strong of a history as the national anthem has to the United States, it shares a unique history with baseball.
