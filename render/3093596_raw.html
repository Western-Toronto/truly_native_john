Garmin Edge GPS Bike Computer Sales and Rebates

14 Apr
2014
Posted in: Bike Gear , Riding Life
By Eric No Tweets

For most of us, the early spring months mark the beginning of our cycling season. Why not track all those miles and routes with a Garmin Edge GPS? Bike computers have come a long way from types that rely on the spoke mounted magnet. A Garmin Edge can obviously track speed and distance similar to the traditional type. But if those metrics are all youre interested in, do you need GPS? Technically no. You can still use your old bike computer, still talk on your rotary phone and still record Wheel Of Fortune on your VCR. But once you make the leap and immerse yourself in possibilities of a Garmin Edge computer, youll never go back. Pair that device to Garmin Connect or Strava (my personal favorite) and you have the perfect platform to track everything you could ever need or want.

ANT+ and Bluetooth technology allow these devices to wirelessly record cadence, power, heart rate, while having the ability to upload the files instantly through your Android or iPhone. The built in altimeter function will record your altitude as well as vertical gain and calculate current grade. Lastly the GPS can provide not only the speed/distance, but also create a map of your route. Its beautiful!

Garmin Edge 1000

The top of the line Garmin is the newly released Edge 1000 . With live maps and routing, wi-fi capabilities, this is the Rolls Royce of bike computers. It is a little bigger in size, but go big or go home, right? At $600, you may need to borrow from your beer fund.

  • Large 3.0 Display See what you need with ease, and stay focused on the road ahead. Edge 1000 uses a glove-friendly, touchscreen Display that adjusts automatically for low-light areas, such as shade and tunnels
  • Segments Turn every ride into a race by competing on Garmin Connect Segments and seeing Real-time results on your Edge 1000, including alerts for segment start and finish and leaderboard rankings
  • Smart Notifications See your text, email and call Notifications from your iPhone 4s or later while riding
  • Bike-Specific Navigation -Navigate the best cycling roads with preloaded maps and points of interest (POIs). Round-trip Routing makes it easy to explore new roads, and Route Planner allows you to create routes using the map or POIs directly on the device
  • Includes: Edge 1000, extended out-front mount, standard mount, preloaded maps, tether, USB cable, manuals

Garmin Edge 810 $100 Rebate

Essentially the predecessor and now little brother to the Edge 1000. The Edge 810 still has maps and remains a highly functional do it all device. Currently, Garmin is offering a $100 rebate on an Edge 810 through May 31st, 2014. That lowers the price down to $400 and makes this a very inciting deal.

  • Garmin 810 computer process data stream so it saves your phones battery, leaving you with plenty of juice for an emergency
  • Improved navigation ability uses onboard maps and bikes you bike friendly routing
  • Additional street or topographical maps can be downloaded from Garmin and loaded onto the Garmin
  • microSD card slot for expanded data storage or loading Garmin maps

  • Compatible with ANT+ enabled devices and power meters
  • GPS + pressure sensitive altimeter gives you extra precise ascent and descent data
  • HotFix satellite prediction system improves signal acquisition

Garmin Edge 510

This is the updated version of the original Edge 500. If you dont need the map feature this is the device for you. Slightly smaller than the 810, it still has Bluetooth and ANT+ and will do just about everything you need it to do (minus the maps). I currently use this device, and its incredibly simple and accurate. Starting at $330, its getting more pocket friendly, but with the current rebate on the 810, Id probably go that route.

Garmin Edge 500 35% Off

While this may be a discontinued model, you can get huge savings and still have a high functioning device. If youre wanting a GPS device and the most bang for your buck, pick this one up. Currently through Nashbar , you can grab this for 35% off. Still has ANT+ so you can use a 3rd party power meter and heart rate monitor. You loose the touchscreen feature and Bluetooth of the other devices. However, the $180 price tag makes that a non-issue. Calling this device basic is probably a crime in the state of Kansas. I owned this prior to the 510 and still failed to use all of its functionality.
