    Person of the Day

    Drone strike kills ISIS recruiter in Afghanistan

    By Olivia Prentzel February 09, 2015 Follow Olivia Prentzel on Twitter

    Tweet

    Back in September, this plane was used for air strikes in Syria to target ISIS. Now, the U.S. is using drones in Afghanistan to target the terrorists. (US Military / Flickr)


    Abdul Rauf , the top recruiter for ISIS, was killed when a drone targeted his car in Afghanistan. Five other militants were killed in the attack as well. His car was transporting ammunition and explosives when it was targeted.

    Rauf was a former Taliban commander who became ISIS's "deputy emir" in Afghanistan with a job to form his own group of insurgents. He was captured by U.S. forces in December 2001 (and held in Guantanamo Bay) and released to Afghan custody in December 2007, and then freed in 2009.

    " #IS leader in #Afghanistan & former #Taliban governor, Mullah Abdul Rauf, reportedly killed in drone strike. pic.twitter.com/SLsi1Bt25l "

    Maqsood Asi (@MaqsoodAsi) February 9, 2015

    This was the first known U.S.-led drone attack in Afghanistan against the terrorist group.
