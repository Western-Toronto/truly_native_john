The Most Famous Stamps in History

As with most collectibles, certain stamps can accumulate massive price tags. Whether they are rare because they were part of a very limited set or because they feature a design/printing error, some stamps have gained a legendary status throughout the years. Below, we take a look at some of the most famous stamps in historyif you happen to have one of these in your collection, it might be time to get very, very excited!

The Penny Black (1840)
This was the first pre-paid postage stamp ever, issued by the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland. While this stamp isnt particularly rare, one in good condition will fetch you between 140 and 1,800. However, one versionbearing a red Maltese cross cancellationonce sold for a staggering $2.4 million at auction.

The Basel Dove (1845)
This stamp was the first and only issued by the city of Basel, Switzerland. The Basel Dove was also the first to be printed in three coloursblue, black and crimsonand is now valued at approximately 11,000.

The Blue Penny (1847)
This has the honour of being the most valuable stamp ever sold in the UK after fetching more than 1 million at auction in 2011. The Blue Penny was one of the first stamps issued for the Mauritius Island postal serviceof the 200 stamps produced, just 26 still exist. The Post Office annotation on the left is believed to be a mistake, as others from the time featured the term Post Paid, which was added to the subsequent version of this stamp.

The US Benjamin Franklin Z-Grill (1868)
Widely considered to be Americas rarest stamp, the Z-grill has only two known copies in existenceand just one of these is sold between collectors. In 2005, a collector swapped his block of four Inverted Jennies (see below)for which he paid nearly $3 millionfor this stamp. Why so valuable? This is down to the inclusion of the z-grill. A grill was a method of combating counterfeiting, in which a specific pattern of minute squares is embossed on the paper before being absorbed into the paperthe z-grill was the first to be used.

The Inverted Jenny (1918)
When a set of airmail postage stamps bearing an image of the Curtis JN-4 biplane was issued by the United States, a simple printing error became one of the most famous in the history of stamps: the Jenny biplane is printed upside down. While three sheets of these stamps were destroyed, others survived. They passed through various hands over the decades, amassing greater and greater prices, until financier William H. Gross bought a block of four for $2,970,000 (which he later swapped for the aforementioned Benjamin Franklin Z-grill).
