What You Can Learn from the 8 Best and Worst Rebrands of 2014

by Jenna Markowski - 14 Comments

in Advertising , Branding , Entrepreneur Resources

  • Tweet

  • SumoMe

Here at QLP were in the business of putting your logo on cool stuff. Since we see THOUSANDS of logos come across our desks every day, we like to think we know a thing or two about logo design and branding.

2014 saw a lot of companies launching major rebrands. Brands like Southwest Airlines, Pizza Hut, Olive Garden, AirBnB, Netflix, and many others all released new logos and branding materials last year.

Considering how quickly logo design trends come and go, I think now is the perfect time to reflect on the rebrands of last year to see what goes into a successful rebrand and what to avoid in your own rebranding efforts.

So without further ado, lets get right to the winning rebrands from 2014 first! Yeah, usually people like to save the best for last, but today were saving the worst for last!

The best rebrands of 2014

Southwest Airlines

Southwests rebrand is definitely my favorite of 2014. The new logo and wordmark may not be revolutionary by any means, but the new heart logo breathes new life not only into the Southwest brand, but into air travel in general.

Typically when you think of the airport and flying in a plane you think of long security lines, sitting in a boring airport for hours, cramped leg space and arm rests, and screaming babies. Many airline brands do very little to change your perceptions of the air travel experience. However, this new Southwest brand identity aims to put customer service and quite literally put heart into the air travel industry, and I think theyve succeeded.

Check out one of the commercials for the rebranded Southwest below!

Southwests rebrand not only accurately captures the brands mission, but it also sets the brand apart from the competition as Armin from Brand New points out , no other airlines feature hearts in their logos.Southwests new brand identity should inspire brands to create logos that are completely unique to them. In addition, your branding materials shouldnot only reflect the spirit of your company but also the way you want your customers to feel when they experience your brand.

Pizza Hut

In November of last year Pizza Hut unveiled a bold new brand identity and logo, including the new Flavor of Now menu. Together with a rebrand and new menu items (like 10 new crust flavors, sauces, and premium ingredients), Pizza Hut hopes to put their brand back in the forefront of everyones mind when it comes to pizza.

By launching a vibrant new brand identity in tandem with exciting new menu offerings (pretzel pizza crust, anyone??), Pizza Hut was able to get consumers excited about their brand again. You can see more of how Pizza Hut has upped their game with their branding AND their pizza in the commercial below.

Pizza Huts rebrand is pretty drastically different than their old branding materials, which is great! Pizza Hut took a risk and totally revamped their brand to make it more modern and it paid off. However, keeping their well-known rooftop imagery as part of the logo helps prevent them from completely alienating their customers. If youre launching a rebrand, dont be afraid to take risks, but also remember that people hate change, so dont get too crazy and scare your current customers away!

Disney Channel

I dont know how many of you watch Disney Channel on a regular basis, but as an avid Disney Channel fan I absolutely love the channels new logo. According to Sam Thielman at Adweek , The original logo started life as a temporary fix when widescreen television sets became the norm, and managed to stick for years. Now Disney has given the logo more thought and given the logo a shiny new update that is still consistent with the brand.

The new logo is not a drastic change by any means, but its a welcome change from the old logo that was starting to look a little outdated. The most important part of the logo is how it works within Disney Channels ubiquitous magic wand station promos.

You can see the new logo in action in some of Disney Channels station identification spots below.

Disney Channel was able to launch a new logo without losing any of the channels magic. When you rebrand your company, start by identifying your companys core values and missions so that when you create a new logo and identity all of your branded elements still maintain your brands spirit.

Olive Garden

Placing Olive Gardens rebrand among the best rebrands of last year may be a polarizing opinion, considering how heated conversations in the design community were about Olive Gardens new logo. Many designers panned the new logo , but many others came to Olive Gardens defense! The 99designs community voted Olive Gardenthe best dining chain rebrand of 2014.

All of that said, I think now that the dust has settled and weve all gotten over our gut reaction of hating all change, its safe to say that this new logo for Olive Garden is a huge step in the right direction for the brand. While the new logo loses some of the homemade, family-oriented, artisanal vibe of the old logo, the updated type treatment and olive sprig give the brand a welcome update and make the logo feel more fresh and modern.

Olive Gardens brand renaissance (as it was referred to in Dardens PowerPoint presentation for investors) includes not just a new logo, but new designs for Olive Garden restaurants and new menu offerings as well. You can read more about the rebrand and see more pictures of Olive Gardens brand elements in Dardens PowerPoint here .

The takeaway: While its true that not everyone loved the new Olive Garden logo, everybody was talking about it!Any publicity is good publicity right? You can make a big splash with your rebrand by unveiling a drastic overhaul of your brands identity and sticking to your guns.We can learn a lot from the best rebrands and new logos of 2014, but we can probably glean even more tips from the brands that missed the mark. Moving right along, lets take a look at the worst rebrands of 2014!

The worst rebrands of 2014

Monster.com

When Monster.com launched their new logo last July they launched it with an animated version of the flag design you see above, which admittedly was a pretty cool concept!

That said, this one is on the list of the worst rebrands not because the logo is inherently bad, but because of the application. I actually quite like the new flag logo and it is very eye-catching and dynamic even on paper. However, as Mark Wilson pointed out in a roundtable discussion for Fast Company , the animated version of the new logo is nowhere to be found on Monsters website, which is arguably the best place to use an animated logo.

Whereas Monsters new logo looks sharp and modern on print materials, unfortunately the sites website is stuck with a wordmark that falls flat.

When you are designing your new logo consider all of the mediums available to you and how your logo will be applied with each one. Monster.com missed a huge opportunity in not using their animated logo on their website. In fact, not using the animated logo on the web nearly renders the whole concept pointless!

Hershey

The new logo for Hersheys made a big splash last September when many readers discovered that the new Hersheys kiss in the logo looks an awful lot like Apples famous poop emoji . Woops!

Poop emoji aside (man, I never thought Id get to write the word poop so many times in a blog post before!), the new flat design of the Hersheys logo loses some of the character and charm of the old logo. Personally, I think this new logois too corporate for a company that sells chocolate!

When you launch your new logo you want everyone to be talking about how awesome your new brand identity is, not how much it literally resembles a turd (or any other weird thing that the internet could conjure up). You can try to avoid that mistake by getting as many eyes as possible on your logo before you launch it. Whether you hold meetings internally or bring in focus groups, hopefully between several sets of eyes you can avoid a major slip-up!

Airbnb

The launch of Airbnbs new logo in 2014 was surrounded in much controversy. First many people claimed that the new logo looked like a vagina , penis , butt basically name any naughty bit and people can see the resemblance in Airbnbs logo .

Then some in the design community discovered that Airbnbs new logo bared a striking resemblance to the logo of Automation Anywhere . However, that claim was later explained as a coincidence in a joint statement from Airbnb and Automation Anywhere.

All of those controversies aside, to me the most offensive part about the new Airbnb logo is how heavy-handed and borderline pretentious the explanation for the symbol is. Airbnb has named their new symbol The Blo. In order to understand what the new symbol means you can watch the video below.

The new symbol doesnt convey anything about the brand to me at first glance. If you dont already know what Airbnb is, it is an online community for people to list and book travel lodging around the world. Can you tell that from the new logo?

Well, for starters maybe make sure that your logo doesnt look like something that could be misconstrued as vulgar. Second, do your homework and try to create a logo that is not nearly identical to someone elses. Most importantly, though, dont make your message too convoluted. Consumers should be able to tell what your logo means and how it represents your brand upon looking at it. A video nearly 2 minutes long should not be necessary in order for people to understand what your logo signifies. So dont over think it!

Netflix

The best word to describe Netflixs new logo is anti-climactic. As Mark Wilson said in his review of the new logo in Fast Company , The new Netflix logos only real crime is that it has no excuse to exist.

Netflix is among several brands that jumped on the flat design trend last year. The new logo will be used not only on the Netflix website but also in the title cards and marketing for Netflix original programming.

The new Netflix logo is certainly inoffensive, but theres also not really anything notable about it at all. Similar to the new Hersheys logo, his is another case of the flat design trend causing a brands logo to lose some of its character.

If youre going through the trouble of rebranding or creating a new logo, you might as well go big or go home, right? Sometimes subtlety works, but in this case the changes are so small many were left wondering, Why bother? Rebranding is a great opportunity to get people talking about your brand, so make sure you create something worth talking about!

In 2014 we saw some inspiring rebrands, but we also saw some duds. As you go forward planning your brand strategy for 2015 and onward, you can take inspiration and tips from the successful and not-so-successful rebrands of last year. And if youre anything like me, youre looking forward to seeing which brands release new logos and identities in 2015!

What do you think? Did I miss any of your favorite rebrands from 2014? Do you disagree with any of my choices? What else can we learn from the rebrands of 2014? Sound off in the comments below!
