Top Beer and Food Festivals in June

Food & Beer NYC Festivals Worth Checking Out This June


By: Michael Illas

535
1

The amount of things to do in New York are already endless, and now that summers here that number just increased. From free concerts to movie screenings, theres always something worth doing in the city during the summer, including festivals that revolve around food, beer and live music. Heres a list of festivals that are worth messing up your summer physique for

Taste of Times Square (June 2)



This event might be the most G-Rated festival out of the ones mentioned. If you thought Times Square couldnt get more crowded, brace yourself. This event that is located on 46th Street in-between Broadway and 9th Avenue from 5:00 PM - 9:00 PM, and will offer food from over 40 restaurants and music. The event admission is free, although each taste ticket will cost you $1.00 and dishes range from $1.00 - $6.00. If youre the type of person who doesnt get paranoid in crowded places, Id recommend passing by, it might be a good way to start your summer.

Big Apple BBQ Block Party (June 7 & 8)



Southern comfort moved up north. Big Apple BBQ Block Party is a great time to enjoy (a lot of) southern food, beer and live music. The event is located at Madison Square Park from 11:00 AM - 6:00 PM.

The event itself is free to get in, but, food and beverages might be kind of expensive if youre a broke college student (like I was when I first experienced this festival), so if you plan on eating or drinking Id recommend bringing a few bucks. There are some options regarding VIP tickets as well. The FastPass ($125.00/day), which includes $100 value redeemable for food and beverages and access to express lines for barbecue. Then, theres the BigPiggin VIP Pass ($265.00/day), which includes exclusive access to a new VIP Hospitality area of Madison Square Park that essentially includes a complimentary buffet, a day long open bar, air-conditioned restrooms, a front-porch, along with the $100 value redeemable for food and beverages and access to express lines for barbecue.

If youre into meat, southern food, live music, and of course beer, the Big Apple BBQ Block Party is definitely something you should check out with your friends. If youre a hardcore vegetarian who gets sick of the smell (or view) of meat, Id recommend sitting this one out.

Where the Wild Beers Are (June 7)



Are you one of those people who like to order beers no one has ever heard of? Do people often question what you brought when you bring a six-pack to a friends house warming party? Then this event is perfect for you! Where the Wild Beers Are is located in (you guessed it!) Brooklyn, 50 Coffey Street, Red Hook from 1:00 PM - 5:00 PM to be more specific. Space is limited so you have to register on their website and you receive drink tickets by the amount of beers you bring, so its sort of like a BYOB, but other people can drink your beer and you can drink other peoples beers. Id recommend reading the FAQ before showing up.

The only beers that will be allowed at the event are sour or wild beers, so if youre picky with beer this isnt for you (sorry). If youre a beer enthusiast or if you simply enjoy drinking, this would be a unique BYOB worth experiencing.

Beerfields (June 21)



Okaythis is in Long Islandstill close (and awesome) enough to make the list! The festival will be held at the Pennysaver Amphitheater in Farmingville, NY from 4:00 PM and the tickets can be found on Ticketmaster starting at $65.30. The event fuses music and (lots of) beer in an outdoor setting. Beerfields focuses on catering to the masses by providing education and exposing the wonders of craft beer while entertaining the people with top notch music festival performances. This festival is definitely worth taking the LIRR out to Long Island.

NYC Craft Beer Festival - Summer International (June 20 & 21)



NYC Craft Beer Festival is possibly the Comic Con of beer festivals. It will be held on 68 Lexington Avenue (between 25th and 26th Street) and there are three sessions for the festival, from 2:00 PM - 4:30 PM and another from 7:00 PM - 9:30 PM on Saturday, June 20, and 8:00 PM - 10:30 PM on Sunday, June 21. Prices start at $55.00 GA and $75.00 VIP (an extra hour) for each session. There will be over 150 craft beer selections, seminars and souvenirs, which are all included in the admission. This event is highly recommended for beer enthusiasts, fraternity members and anyone who enjoys a nice cold beer.
