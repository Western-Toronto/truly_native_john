Is this a new fantasy game format?

Yes, we created it! Our goal was to make ScoreStreak simple to understand and join, while still requiring skill and strategy. The more you play, the more you will understand that.

If I can pick any player to my lineup, wont everyone pick the same players?

Of course there are some players that most users will seriously consider for a slot in their lineup, but some objectives in ScoreStreak are difficult for even the best players so there is strategy in not only whom you slot, but also to which goals you slot them.

Can I achieve a perfect score?

Yes, but it is really, really, really rare! If all nine players in your lineup achieve their goals, you will score a perfect 81 points. See the How it Works section for an example.

What is Bonus Cash?

Bonus Cash is offered to users upon their first deposit, as a reward for referring other new users (must deposit at least $10), and during special promotional offers. Your Bonus Cash balance is displayed on your Profile page and is released gradually after you enter and complete paid contests.

How fast does Bonus Cash become real cash?

Bonus Cash is released as real cash at a rate of 4% of the entry fee of the contest you enter. For example, if you enter a $50 contest, $2 of Bonus Cash will be released into your main funds account once the contest goes live. If a contest or entry is canceled, your entry fee will be refunded and no bonuses will be earned.

Why cant I use my Bonus Cash to directly enter contests?

Gradually releasing your Bonus Cash as you play prevents fraud and removes incentives to create multiple accounts (thats against our Terms). Note that you can never withdraw Bonus Cash as cash.

Can I change my lineup between when I enter and when my games start?

Yes, go to the My Games section and you can change players in your lineup until your contest begins. Once your contest begins, you will be able to see everyones lineups, and all players are locked.

After I have entered a contest, can I cancel?

No, once you have entered your entry fee, you are committed to the contest. If, however, a non-guaranteed contest does not fill all of its entries, it will be cancelled and your entry fee will be refunded to your account.

What happens if one of my players is injured?

An injured player earns you zero points. We provide available injury information next to each player, but we recommend that you always check (ScoreStreak and 3rd party sites) for player injuries before your game begins.

Do contest scores update during the games?

NFL, MLB, and NBA categories update every few minutes during the games.

How are scores calculated?

Stats are provided by our statistics partner, SportsData, LLC. Sometimes official stats are adjusted within the scoring period by SportsData, LLC. But once the scoring period is over no stats will be changed and ScoreStreak results are final.

What if there is a statistical error that affects my contest?

All players' stats are final shortly after completion of each game. Any changes made after the final box score is submitted will not be taken into account.

What happens if a game is canceled or rained out?

If a game is rained out and not made up within a contest's scoring period, then all players in that game will receive 0 points. Make sure to check the weather to avoid picking players from a rained out game!

What if there is a tie?

At the end of a contest, if you tie for first place or meet one of the other winning conditions, you will divide the prize. For example, if you tie with another user for 1st place in a $90 prize contest where winner takes all, you will each earn $45.

Whats the difference between a daily contest and a weekly contest?

Daily contests occur in a single day while weekly contests include more than a single day. For example, a weekly NFL contest might allow you to select players from the Thursday, Sunday and Monday games, while a daily NBA contest would include only the games from that single day.

Can I play from my phone, tablet and PC?

Yes! We built it from the ground up for all three devices so that you can have a great experience no matter which device you are using to play.

Is entering contests with real money legal?

Yes. Fantasy sports that involve contests of skill are generally not subject to the Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA). We take this law very seriously, and there are several U.S. states that we do not allow to deposit or withdraw real money. Read more here .

Are you a shady offshore company?

No. We are based in San Mateo, CA, about 30 minutes south of San Francisco.

How do I deposit funds?

Its easy! Click the deposit button from the home page and select the method that you want to use. You can use credit cards and PayPal. Just follow the on screen instructions. We pay the transaction fees so there is no additional cost for you to deposit.

Are my deposits safe?

Yes, we take great care to make the process of depositing funds secure, and we keep all customer funds in a separate bank account from our company operating account ensuring that your funds will be safe.

How do I withdraw funds?

Just click Withdraw from your Account page and follow the simple instructions. We use PayPal for withdrawals and plan to add additional options. Just enter the amount that you want to withdraw and the request will be made. Withdrawal requests will usually be processed within 48 hours. We reserve the right to investigate any activity to prevent fraud.

Are there any withdrawal fees?

PayPal fees may apply. If they do, you will be charged 2.9% of your withdrawal + $.30 (that is PayPals fee). For example, if you withdraw $100 you would be charged $3.20 for the transaction, so $96.80 would be added to your account.

Are my earnings taxable?

Any request that brings your total earnings (withdrawals less deposits) over $600 for the calendar year will require a valid address and Social Security Number prior to processing. This information will be used to file a from 1099-MISC at the end of the year.

I see some contests use Coins. What are they?

We give all new users Coins to use for non-real money contests. You can join those contests for practice or fun. Note that unlike real money, you cannot withdraw Coins as they have no cash value. People who live in U.S. states that we prohibit from entering real money contests (due to regulations in those states) can use coins.
