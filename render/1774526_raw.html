Are You a Bully?

Follow us and your life will never be the same again (potentially)

If someone asked you whether you were a bully what would you say? Most people would probably say no. After all, the word bully conjures up images of Nelson from The Simpsons; tipping kids upside down for their lunch money and beating up anyone a bit different. However, thanks to the internet there are now far more ways someone can be a bully. Im willing to bet that over half of the people reading this post will have done one of the things Im going to talk about. I know Im guilty of at least one in the past. Read through the list below and then answer the question again Are you a bully?

Naming and Shaming

This is something Ive begun to see a lot of on Facebook and other social media recently albeit from the younger generation more than the older generations. This is when someone thinks they should name and shame someone who has supposedly done something wrong. One of the most popular name and shame posts are about men and women (mostly women) who are rumoured to have been sleeping around. Someone all high and mighty will call them up on it, call them a dirty slag or a diseased player, and everyone will have their two cents on the matter. One I saw recently was about a girl who was supposedly drinking whilst she was pregnant; something which I think is terribly wrong if true, I hasten to add. 136 people liked this status and countless others commented on it, voicing their opinion on the matter. What worried me is that this girl sounds like she needs help and support more than anything. After all, you cant be in a very good place if you are drinking whilst pregnant. Instead, shes got people writing statuses about her and calling her every name under the sun. Some people even told her to kill herself. Bullies. If youve ever taken part in one of these statuses (even if its just a like) then youre a bully too.

Ganging Up

Heres something I saw recently on the profile picture of a very pretty girl and a young man who was clearlyjust trying to compliment her:

Young man: You look beautiful

Pretty girl: Looool (tags all of her friends) thanks creeeeeep.

Young man: Sorry, wasnt trying to be a creep. Weve been friends on Facebook for ages, just thought Id say you look beautiful.

Cue all of the girls friends and the girl going through his profile to pick out all of this guys flaws Glasses, spots, greasy hair, bushy eyebrows, no friends, nerd, dork, virgin, the list was endless.

What the actual ****people?! Firstly, if this guy really was a creep then why accept his friend request in the first place? If the girl thought this guy was a creep then she should have just deleted him off of Facebook and removed his comment. But no, she decided to enlist the help of her minions and destroy this poor guy. I dread to think how that young man felt afterwards, but I just wanted to give him a hug. There is absolutely no need to go through his profile and bring up every single one of his insecurities. Bullies. If youve ever taken part in one of these ganging up scenarios (or tagged all your mates) then youre a bully too.

Memes

Now this is a difficult one and probably where the majority of you are going to be caught out.You know those memes you share about ugly girls, geeky guys, slaggy women, deformed looking men, makeup fails, parenting fails and all the rest of it? Those pictures, theyre of real people. Of course, there are a few people who volunteer their pictures up or create fake pictures in order to become an internet sensation. However, the majority of people whose photos youre sharing around the internet are being bullied. They may have taken those photos for their blog or a social media profile, those photos have then been taken by someone and turned into a joke. The person who owns the original photo then has to watch it whizzing around the internet, being mocked by millions. Imagine how that would make you feel? People tag their friends, share on their walls and just generally take the utter p*ss out of that person. Bullies. If you tag your friends or share those photos then youre a bully too.

So, are you a bully?

Every day there are people who suffer and even take their own lives because of cyber bullying. Think twice the next time you make someone the butt of your online joke or get involved in something that you think could be cyber bullying. There could be more damage caused than you can ever imagine. Try to think how it would feel if the shoe was on the other foot.

Please take a look through the websites below for more information and helplines.

Remember to share and ask your friends Are you a bully?
