The Grand Parkway Project: Improving Transportation in One of the Fastest-Growing Cities in the U.S.

Supporting Efforts to Pave the Way for Better Transportation Around Houston

The Houston metropolitan area ranks first in population growth among major metropolitan areas with a minimum population of 50,000, and second in job growth nationwide. It is home to 26 Fortune 500 companies. As businesses and job seekers flock to the city, the transportation system has become strained and congested. To keep progress in motion, the Grand Parkway Transportation Corporation is constructing the Grand Parkway System, part of the larger Grand Parkway Project, a 184-mile highway surrounding the greater Houston area.

The Grand Parkway Project, supported by the Grand Parkway Transportation Corporation and the Texas Department of Transportation, will link suburban communities with major roadways, allowing Houston to better accommodate its rapidly growing population and economy. The project will improve safety and road conditions for the community, create about 1,200 construction jobs, and eventually provide much-needed additional roadway capacity to help alleviate increased traffic demand due to population and employment growth.

Goldman Sachs served as the sole manager of $2.9 billion in Grand Parkway System Toll Revenue Bonds that financed the Grand Parkway System. The issuance was the largest municipal securities offering of 2013. As part of the effort, we organized a multi-city investor roadshow that attracted 125 bond investors for the project. In our role as underwriter, our investment banking professionals worked closely with the Grand Parkway Transportation Corporation, its financial advisors and counsel for more than a year to assist in structuring the financing.
