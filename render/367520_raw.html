Why Adventure-Seekers and the Young at Heart MUST Travel to Norway


Why Adventure-Seekers and the Young at Heart MUST Travel to Norway


By DPG Staff
March 12, 2014

Attention all adventure-seekers have you ever been to Norway? Whether youre an Oslo regular or youve never stepped foot anywhere near the Arctic Circle, its safe to assume that you wont regret a trip to Norway if youre looking to experience firsthand some of the worlds most incredible landscapes.

Unknown | CC BY 2.0
Here, you can snowmobile upon frozen fjords while brilliant northern lights dance above your head, kayak azure waterways cradled between sheer cliffs and snowshoe across massive glaciers all in a 24-hour period. Luckily, you get to choose how you experience Norway and that may be no easy feat once you see your options:

Dog-sledding | One of the best ways to explore the Norways frozen landscapes is on a husky-drawn sledge. Travel over the snow-covered hills of the Finnmark island of Kvalya and surrounding Troms area and take in the wintery glory of the Arctic Circle on one of the areas most traditional forms of transportation.

Birding | Have you seen a puffin in person? Outdoor enthusiasts flock (like seabirds) to Norways northern coast to catch glimpses of Finnmarks largest clusters of puffins, plus plenty of other coastal birds, including sea eagles and razorbills. During the fall, youre bound to spot Europes smallest seabird, the British storm-petrel.

Crab-catching | If you really want to experience Norways unique wildlife, try a crab-catching adventure during the summer or winter. Pull enormous king crabs straight from the Barents Sea and enjoy the succulent seafood signature to Norway. Some king crabs weigh more than 30 pounds and have a leg span of approximately 6 feet!

Kayaking | Norways fjords are definitely pretty to look at, but have you ever considered kayaking them? Experience the sea in an extreme way by taking to the waters around Troms. Float in a sea kayak below jagged cliffs jutting hundreds of feet into the air and watch for Norways fascinating marine wildlife on the way.

Horseback-riding | Norways Lofoten Islands are known for their natural beauty what better way to see for yourself then on horseback? The Gimsy Hov Horse Farm offers guided tours on Icelandic steeds. On horseback, youll have the chance to ride along the sandy coastline and see the islands inspiring Viking-age relics.
