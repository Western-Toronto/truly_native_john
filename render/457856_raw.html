The Startup Of Getting The Job You Want After College

January 20, 2014 Christina Mendel Leave a comment

The first day after college is the first day of the rest of your life. Make it count!

Will your life be worth making a movie about?

Getting the job you want right out of college is two things: possible and important. If you are reading this post, chances are you are habitually giving 100% and arent satisfied with less than living up to your potential. The issue with that is that if you dont get your dream job, it will make you extremely unhappy but if you do get your dream job you will put yourself under extreme pressure.

The worst thing that happens to a lot of talented students is to have an exact blueprint of what the job will be like and then the job turns out to be different because the team isnt motivated or the corporate culture isnt a good fit. I have made a list of things that I believe are essential to get the job you want and I have included affiliate links of products I support.

The key to getting your dream job is to make a blueprint of where you are headed and to prepare accordingly.

#1 Skills

Choose someone whose job looks like your dream job long-term. For instance, if you would like to work in fashion, your ideal job might be to be Anna Wintours personal assistant or to be a buyer for Elle. The obvious thing to do would be to look at skill set requirements of entry positions but thats not whats going to get you the job. Knowing what you need to be a valuable addition to the company long-term on the other hand will!

  • What skills do you need to acquire to be a buyer/get your long-term dream job?
  • How long does that usually take?
  • Who in the company is the decision maker for that job and how can you get close?
  • How can I get the skills I need to get started on this path?

#2 Money

Earning a 6 figure income isnt a lot of money. Just because it puts you statistically in the top 3% of the population doesnt mean you can enjoy financial freedom. From what I have seen, most people make the mistake of setting themselves a minimum of what they would be willing to accept, e.g. I wont go lower than $45k/year. From where I stand, I would also work a year for free if it helps me to make a million the year after.

  • Its important to set yourself a long-term financial goal, for instance being a millionaire under 30.
  • Be aware whether the job category you are looking into typically leads to the kind of income you are looking to make
  • Be aware that any successful individuals assets do not come from only one source of income, for instance you can invest a third of your salary and create an additional income stream from the returns.
  • Know why you want a certain amount of money. To travel the world? Pride? Prestige? To start your own company? To pay back your student loans? To own an apartment? If you dont know why you want something, you wont have the motivation to stick to your goal.

#3 Presentation

Imagine you would be presenting the limited edition of a great service that nobody knows about. What characteristics are unique to you? Most people think there is nothing special about them because they have the same skill set as everybody else they studied with (at least on paper) but thats not true. Your resume is like your fingerprint, it is unique and should represent you consistently.

Choose a font that best represents you. Futura, Georgia, Arial, Verdana, Times New Roman, Helvetica, Didot, Open Sans all represent something different. Select a maximum of two fonts and stick to them. If you have a blog, use those fonts. Put them on your networking card, on your resume, use them for your referrals, for your email, in your email signature, in your portfolio of papers.

Ah, yes. Your portfolio . The one thing nobody else will have and nobody else can compete with. Treat your job interview like a startup and show the work that has been officially evaluated by professors. Print all the papers you ever wrote that got good marks and bind them as a portfolio. Use the same fonts. Doing that is such a simple thing to do but it actually gives you a major competitive advantage because you are actively avoiding direct comparison. What are the chances that someone is applying to the same job who also had the same classes at the same time with the same professor and wrote about the same topics? They are minimal!

Choose your colors . If you think thats ridiculous, choose black. That also says something. If you are into design, choose black and one other color and use it for headings (e.g your name on the resume and the titles of your paper in the portfolio). I think we would all agree that you would have higher chances of getting hired as part of a branding team if you actually showed that you understood the consistent representation of a brand and applied it to your application. Make it dark gray if you think its too colorful or have black font with beige paper there are so many ways to stand out!

Spelling and grammar . Must. Be. Flawless.

Dress code and a big smile go a long way. Even if you know that the company is more laid back, wear a suit to the interview to show that you are committed.

#4 Show Effort

Talent isnt worth anything. There. I said it. If you currently dont have any measurable skills like web design, online advertising, programming, search engine optimization, or copywriting to just name a few, you must win by showing you are putting more effort into it than all the other applicants.

  • be early
  • print your resume on thicker paper
  • bring an extra copy of everything
  • have networking cards
  • get business cards from all interviewers
  • send a thank you email to all interviewers within 24 hours (handwritten notes are even better)
  • polished shoes
  • no cleavage for the women and no messy tie for the guys
  • wrist watch
  • no or very little perfume
  • sit after the others sit or when you are asked to
  • shake hands with both hands
  • have a professional laptop bag for your documents, thats the only bag you need
  • get the door for people even if its not the person you are interviewing with
  • dont drink coffee within 1h before the interview
  • have some questions to ask the interviewer about the position like daily tasks and what the next steps are
  • pay attention to detail

#5 Be likable

99% of extremely successful people are great to work with. If you are invited to the interview, they already know that you have the technical skills they are looking for, its now a matter of whether you fit their team. Here are a couple of tips on how to be liked.

In order to be liked, the other person must identify with you.

Without staring at him or her, try to understand what the other person is like. Do the socks match the suit? Expensive wrist watch? Messy hair? Does the interview start late? Where is the interview being held? Red lipstick or natural makeup? All of these things tell you something about the person in front of you and if you understand them quickly, you can phrase your responses accordingly.

If someone pretends to be the man, let him. Say things like someone in a position like yours of course knows that. If there is a woman trying to act all bossy and tough, show that you got it. If someone is busy and mentions it several times or is in a hustle, acknowledge that by saying I can tell that you are really busy, people must appreciate you very much to include you so often.

All behaviors are linked to emotions . It doesnt matter whether your interviewer is actually busy or not it matters whether he or she wants you to know and why. Busy people are popular and requested. Clearly, this person appreciate a comment in that direction.

Last but not least, body language is extremely important. Scientific studies have shown that people who have similar body language like each other best. Obviously, you dont want to mirror every single move instantly but it helps to watch details like how to pull the chair to the table, how to adjust papers in front of you, where to put the arms when sitting, etc. and to choose to imitate two of those observations.
