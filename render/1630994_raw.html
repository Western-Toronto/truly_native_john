Using the Weber Q in Color Gas Grill

May 27th 2015 Gear by by Joe Miragliotta

8

When I think of grilling, I think of good food and the company of my family and friends. I was recently sent a Weber Q 1200 gas grill to try out, and itcouldnt have come at a better time. Recently, my brothermoved into a new home and had his first child. Naturally the entire family was eager to meet the little one, so he invited family and friends over for a cookout. The only problem was he lacked a grill.Well, at least not until now.

The Weber Q 1200 is extremely portable considering its a grill. I was able to pack it in the trunk of my car with very little effort. In the above image you will see how its side tables are folded in, while the bottom shows everything opened up. This makes for very easy storage whether it be in the trunk of your car or your garage.

Eager to test the new grill and kickstart the cookout, I started the Weber Q 1200 up and threw on some hamburgers and hot dogs.Theporcelain-enameled, cast-iron cooking grates kept all of the food cooking evenly, while the grease and loose juices that fell from the cooking grates werecaptured in the easy-to-dispose drip panat the bottom.

Whencooking I used a few otherWeber tools that can be foundon their online store, including the Stainless Steel Two-Piece Portable Tool Set and the Barbecue Mitt .

All-in-all, the Weber Q in Color grill was a win! It was a realpleasure to have something so simple and cool looking perform as well as the stationary grills Ive used in the past. The Weber Q 1200 comes in a variety ofdifferent colors, including fuchsia, black (my personal choice), blue, purple, orange, white, green, and titanium.

So if yourein the market for a portable grill, I would highly recommend you considering the Q 1200.Im already looking forward to taking it along with me this summer when I go camping in Northern California.
