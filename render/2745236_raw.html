Pregnancy and Oral Health

October 7th, 2014

Many mothers will often wonder, What does pregnancy have to do with my oral health?

Well, the answer is quite simple: quite a lot! In fact, dental health problems during pregnancy can be a sign of other health problems. Your oral health routine is of utmost importance during pregnancy, and should be seen as equally important as a healthy diet and regular check-ups with your dentist.

Pregnancy can bring about many changes to your body, and dental problems that may not have been an issue before pregnancy may soon uncover themselves when youre expecting. Hormone changes, morning sickness, sensitivity to medications All these things affect your teeth in numerous ways, and can increase your likelihood of developing gingivitis, granulomas and pregnancy-induced diabetes.

Fortunately, many of these health problems are not usually serious and many are quite uncommon. Many of them can, however, bring quite a bit of discomfort and in some cases cause more serious health problems if not treated. Yes, flossing, brushing and mouthwash can all help, but can cause other problems as well if not done right.

Therefore, we have created a brand new infographic that outlines the various oral health problems that mothers to be should be aware of when expecting. Should you experience any of the symptoms displayed below, theres no need to worry but we recommend booking an appointment with both your dentist and your GP as soon as you can. Your general health is often linked to your dental health, so its important to speak to both health professionals.

Take a look at the infographic below, and if youre experiencing any other discomfort not covered here, leave a comment or give us a call today.

Therefore, is worth getting thorough, regular check-ups, no matter how good your dental hygiene is.
