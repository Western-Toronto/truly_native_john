4 Things to do before selling a smartphone 6

This entry was posted in Selling articles and tagged sell a smartphone ship a smartphone turn off iMessage Wipe your smartphone on December 17, 2014 by Jon Partridge
0
0
0
0
0

If theres one thing we should all take away from the massive Sony hack , its that you cant be too careful with your security. While none of us are necessarily the target that the multimedia conglomerate is, its still better to be safe than sorry. While beZilch.com wants you to feel safe buying and selling your products, there are steps you can take to minimize the security risks you face. When you sell your smartphone, youre sending a device thats had a large portion of your life to a stranger. Selling a smartphone will be safe 99.9% of the time, but you can increase that to 100% of the time by following these simple steps.

1. Back up your data

As mentioned before, a large portion of your life may be stored on your smartphone. Losing that data would be devastating to many of us. Backing up your phone is very easy, whether you have an iPhone, Android, Windows Phone, or BlackBerry. Backing up your device before you sell it will allow you to retain the purchases you made on that device (apps, media content, etc.) on your new device. Thus, if youre selling your old phone so you can buy a new one, you wont have to purchase all of your apps again.

2. Wipe your device

While losing the content you purchased on your phone would be tough, having it on your phone for its next owner could be more problematic. Would you want a stranger going through your texts and photos? Before selling a smartphone, wiping it clean before you send it off is very important. Doing so will basically make it be just as it was the day you purchased it. iPhones are very easy to wipe, while Android devices can be a little more complicated. When clearing an Android device, an app such as Lookout is recommended to ensure all of the data is wiped properly.

*If youre selling an iPhone, its very important that you turn off iMessagebefore you send it off. Failing to do so will cause text messages to your number to go to your old device.

3. Remove your SIM card

When youre selling a smartphone, make sure to remove the SIM card from it. Your SIM card is what ties your device to your cellular carrier. Even if you dont need that original SIM card for the phone youre replacing that one with, its still a good idea to hang onto it.

4. Erase your memory card

Many phones have small internal memories but include the option to add a MicroSD card. If the phone youre selling has a card youre sending with it, make sure to erase all the data from that card before shipping.

Keeping yourself secure when selling a smartphone isnt anything that needs to be complicated. As long as you follow the above steps, you shouldnt have any trouble. Is there anything we missed? Please dont hesitate letting us know about it in the comments.
