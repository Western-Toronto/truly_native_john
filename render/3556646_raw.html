How To Do Eyeshadow Like a Pro

Mar 9, 2015 Maria

The number one question a beauty blogger gets asked? How to do eyeshadow right! Keep reading for my insider beauty tips on how to master the art of applying eyeshadow.

Choosing Your Eyeshadow Shades

Before you get started on how to do your own eyeshadow, start with selecting the right tones to complement your eye color and your complexion. Earth tones work really well for brown-eyed gals. Choose something with a hint of green in it if you want to make your eyes appear a touch lighter. If you have blue eyes, go for cooler shades like blue or violet.

How To Do Eyeshadow Yourself

If youre a makeup newbie, start by getting your hands on two makeup brushes: a shader brush and a crease brush. Set your three complementary shades out in front of you, and make sure one of them in significantly contrasted. For example, pair some neutral nudes with a dark, shimmering brown.

Using your large shader brush, sweep the lightest shade along your brow bone. If you can find a shade with an iridescent finish, it will help make your eyelid appear wider and your eyebrows more defined.

Next, swipe the medium tone on your lower lid. You can also use the same shade as your brow bone if you want to keep things simple. (See? Learning how to do your eyeshadow like a pro isnt really that hard!)

Once your lighter shades are in, take your crease brush and sweep it along your dark color. Tap the brush gently to shake off excess dust, and then apply your darker color to the crease only. Go back and forth, always gliding the crease brush gently. Pushing it too hard can make for an overly defined line. You want it to blend seamlessly into the other colors.

If you think youre ready to get fancy, dab a smudge brush into a fourth complementary shade and apply in a circular motion to the outer corner of the eyelid only. This is great for highlighting a secondary color. For example, if you have hazel eyes, a greenish shade will bring out the natural green in your eyes.
