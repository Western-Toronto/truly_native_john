Inspired by Ireland: 5 Must-See Places


Inspired by Ireland: 5 Must-See Places


By Lauren Elizondo
June 17, 2013

The Cliffs of Moher, Blarney Stone, Dingle Peninsula what do all of these fascinating places have in common? They are all intrinsically Irish. Whether or not youve traveled to the Emerald Isle, youve probably heard of these iconic landmarks. While words can give you general description, they certainly cannot replace the overwhelming sense of awe inspired by your real life Ireland experiences. Like the exhilarating moment you step up to the sheer cliffs hanging over the Atlantic Ocean while the wind whips around your body, or the feeling of the spongy grass under your feet as you walk the trails along the Ring of Kerry. If a trip to Ireland isnt already on your bucket list, you should jot it down and start counting down the days until you get to see these five must-see places in person:

Shutterstock
Dingle Peninsula | Like the Ring of Kerry, the Dingle Peninsula has earned a reputation for its striking natural beauty. This County Kerry peninsula is part of the westernmost point of the island and sits below Mount Brandon one of Irelands highest peaks. Dingle Peninsula has been called the most beautiful place on earth by National Geographic Traveler.

Shutterstock
The Ring of Kerry | In the county of its namesake, The Ring of Kerry is a 111-mile trail meandering through southwest Ireland that features some of the islands most stunning natural splendor. The route starts in Killarney and passes through several towns and Killarney National Park. Some of the most photographic stops along the way are the expansive Ladies View and the Gap of Dunloe.

Shutterstock
Poulnabrone Dolmen | The curious Poulnabrone Dolmen is actually a County Clare portal tomb dating back to the New Stone Age. Nearly 30 years ago, the remains of approximately 18 adults and 6 children were found, along with personal items , during an excavation to fix a collapsed portal stone. It was thought to have served as a resting place and ritual ceremony stage up to the Celtic Period.

Shutterstock
Blarney Stone | If youve been to the Blarney Stone, youll know that this famous stone is actually part of Blarney Castle near Cork, Ireland. Legend has it that whoever kisses the stone (set in the tower in 1446) will be gifted with the talent of eloquent speech, as well as good luck. Be careful, though to smooch this stone youll have to literally bend over backwards!

Shutterstock
Cliffs of Moher | Jutting up from the coast in County Clare in Burren Region, the Cliffs of Moher are often depicted as the symbol of Ireland. Each year more than 1 million visitors flock to these sheer shale and sandstone rocks rising more than 700 feet into the air. In 1987, they were renamed The Cliffs of Insanity in the cult classic movie The Princess Bride.
