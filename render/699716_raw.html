7 Rules for Being a Kid

May 29, 2014 by Ashley Sears 12 Comments

Summertime is some serious bidness. (Yes, bidness) School is out, and the pools are open, but before you kids get all crazy like on your parents, there are just a few rules that I thought we should lay out to make sure that we do our parenting duties. Make sure you take notes, tattoo them to your forehead backwards (with parental permission of course), and you are sure to have the most epic summer ever.

This is a compenstated post on behalf of the Quality Blue and NickMom, all genius and hilarity are my own.

7 Rules for Being a Kid.

Calling Shotgun: Summer time is FULL of family car trips. Thats why it is ESSENTIAL that you know the ultimate rule in calling shot gun. One must be in sight of the car before calling shot gun. Failure to adhere to this rule will result in immediate infinite noogies.

Swimming in a pool is NOT an excuse not to bathe: We know you are going to be in the pool almost all summer, but just wanted to clarify that the stench of chlorine is not an excuse not to bathe all summer. Now if you bring your shampoo to the pool and USE it, THAT might work (hey, you kill two birds with one stone)

Thou Shalt Not EVER eat the Last Popsicle: Summer time is the perfect time for a popsicle, but unless you want to become the black sheep of the family thou shalt never eat the last popsicle in the house. It doesnt matter if its in there and all freezer burned. If there is only one left, yell for your mom at the top of your lungs or go chase down the ice cream truck (not literally kids, thats how someone loses an eye)

Socks are a Forbidden Accessory During Summertime : It is sacreligious to wear socks during summer time. That is unless you have a sporting event then its a mild excuse. Other than that let those piggies fly free. Spend time instead making lots and lots of sock puppets.

Know the Four Basic Food Groups of Summer: There are only four basic food groups you need to survive during the summertime: hot dogs, watermelon, hamburgers and of course popsicles. All other foods are optional to chow down on during the warmer summer months. (For adults you can add a fifth food group of rum, coconut rum, and/or tequila)

Bedtime, We Dont Need No Stinkin Bed Time: Summertime is the time of NO bed time. Stay up late campout under the stars. Have a blast being a night owl. Just make sure to keep it down so you dont wake mom and dad.

Have a Parent Survival Kit: This is probably THE most important rule on the list! Make sure to pay attention. In order to SURVIVE your summer you must, and I repeat must, keep mom and dad happy. Happy parents equals happy kids. When asking for favors, make sure to bat those beautiful eyes and have smiles on your face. Breakfast in bed wouldnt be a bad idea either. If in doubt, refer to the fifth food group, and learn to make homemade pina coladas. Serve one to each adult and then ask your question. (Trust me it will go over much better this way)
