Amelia Earhart is attempting to fly around the worldagain

Adventures

by Lisa Furgison

Amelia Earhart planned an epic trip back in 1937. She wanted to be the first woman to fly around the world, but 29 days into that trip the famed aviator disappeared over the Pacific Ocean.

Now, more than 75 years later, another adventurous woman, who is also named Amelia Earhart, plans to complete the trip that her eponym couldnt.

Amelia Rose Earhart, a former traffic anchor for an NBC station in Denver, plans to travel 28,000 miles through 14 countries in 18 days in honor of the woman she was named after.

All my life Ive been asked, Is Amelia Earhart your real name? she says. And Im honored to say, Yes. My parents wanted me to have an inspirational name and they certainly picked a good one.

Her name also prompted a lot of people to ask her if she knew how to fly. She decided that when she could save up enough money, shed take a lesson. Her first time in a cockpit was 10 years ago.

I was hooked. I couldnt wait to go back up, she recalls of her first lesson.

Earhart completed her training and got her pilots license, completing her predecessors transcontinental flight from Oakland, California, to Miami as a completion of her training. From that point, Earharts quest to follow in those historic footsteps continued.

I started really considering making an around-the-world flight, she says. Im not a dabbler. When I do something, I do it. So, I decided to find a way to make the trip happen.

She asked Shane Jordan, a skilled flight instructor, to co-pilot. To make the trip a reality, the two had a lot to plan. From plotting out a fuel-appropriate route for the Pilatus PC-12 NG, to getting approval to land in foreign airports, the amount of planning was a bit overwhelming for the new pilot.

I had no idea how much prep work would go into this flight, she says. Weight matters in an aircraft this size so we have to carefully plan which supplies to take. Plus, weve had to make changes to our flight plan based on unforeseen problems. For example, we originally planned to land in Kenya, but fighting broke out there recently so we switched our stop to Tanzania to be safe.

While the planning stages have taken a lot of time, Earhart says she is beyond pumped to take off.

Making this trip is a big commitment, but the excitement and anticipation that I feel right now cant be described.

"The one thing that Ive learned from my namesake is that everyone is entitled to make their own adventure. I want other girls to know they can do the same."

Earharts journey will start on June 26 in Oakland, California, where shell leave from the exact hanger that Amelia Earhart left from seven decades earlier. Shell make two stops in the U.S. before heading east. Shell go through Brazil, Senegal, Tanzania and stop at a series of small, little-known islands that are scattered across the ocean before landing back in Oakland.

The original Earhart got into trouble when she was island hopping. Some people believe she couldnt find one of the small islands she planned to land on in the Pacific Ocean and ran out of fuel, causing her to crash into the ocean. When asked about the original Earharts fate, Earhart says shes armed with more tools and technology that will ensure her safety.

Since Amelia made her flight there have been dramatic improvements in aviation equipment. Weve got GPS, excellent communication tools, and more advanced gear than she had, she says. Im confident in my safety.

Another tool the modern Earhart will have: Wi-Fi. Earhart plans to tweet while shes soaring thousands of miles overhead.

I want people to follow us on this journey. I want to be engaged and be a role model for anyone, especially young girls, who want to take to the skies.

In fact, one of the biggest reasons for this trip is to promote Fly With Amelia Foundation , an organization that Earhart runs to financially support young women who want to fly.

The one thing that Ive learned from my namesake is that everyone is entitled to make their own adventure. I want other girls to know they can do the same.

Flying is my passion.

"When youre soaring through the sky and looking at nothing but breathtaking views, theres nothing else like it.

Once the trip is complete, Earhart plans to stay in the aviation world and run her foundation. She hopes to give flight lessons to teens one day, but before she achieves that goal, shes got a big trip ahead of her.

Flying is my passion. When youre soaring through the sky and looking at nothing but breathtaking views, theres nothing else like it.

You can follow Earharts trip on Facebook and Twitter . You can also watch a live feed of the flight on her website ameliaearhartproject.com
