Transcribing J.S. Bach - Air on a G String

Today we will be using MIDIToTab to transcribe J.S. Bach's famous Air on a G String to guitar.

As with Greensleeves , the transcription contains both the bass and melody lines in one track, so that the result can be played on a single instrument. We will begin with a traditional 6-string guitar tuned to EADGBe. For reasons of brevity, only the first (most popular) section of the piece will be transcribed and presented here.

First, once the MIDI file has been loaded into MIDIToTab and the 6-string guitar selected, we start our first transcription process using Deep Search set to Very High (3rd depth level) and select the Search within all initial positions for a global search.

After the calculations, MIDIToTab finds an optimal solution with a global score of 188 (and a tablature score of 7 ), and evaluates it as Moderate . The exported PDF of the tablature can be seen below.

Importing Guitar Pro files into MIDIToTab

In order to import Guitar Pro files into MIDIToTab , you need to export your Guitar Pro score into MIDI by respecting the following guidelines :


  • Remove all palm mutes and "let ring" effects from the score - Guitar Pro's MIDI export function will significantly modify the original score with palm mutes and "let rings"s: palm mutes are exported as hashed notes and "let ring"s are exported overlapping with other notes and having three times longer duration. Don't worry - removing these effects will not affect the visualization of the score in MIDIToTab.
  • The same with up- or down-striking an entire chord - Guitar Pro will export that chord overlapping with the following notes and chords, this is to be avoided.


Once these guidelines are respected, MIDIToTab can perfectly read, analyze and optimize the MIDI file exported from Guitar Pro.

Transcribing Greensleeves to Guitar and Lute

Today we will be transcribing and optimizing a classic renaissance folk piece, Greensleeves , using MIDIToTab . You can find out more about the piece itself here .

This version of Greensleeves was transcribed to midi using both the chords and the melody lines into one track - the piece can thus be played on a single instrument. Let us first start out with a traditional 6-string guitar tuned to standard EADGBe tuning.

Once the track has been loaded into MIDIToTab , we can see that it is already pretty much optimized, with perhaps a few issues here and there. In order to get a fully optimized tablature we will use Deep Search on Normal setting and choose " Lower on the neck " as a starting position. We do not need deeper searches in this case since after the Normal Deep Search the tablature is already evaluated by MIDIToTab as Easy . The final result is shown below.



You can view the full PDF generated by MIDIToTab here and the corresponding PowerTab file here .

MIDIToTab v1.0 released!

Welcome to the official blog of MIDIToTab - the perfect tool for musicians and composers to optimize tablature or simply to convert midi to tab. This blog will be regularly updated with tips, samples, tutorials and news to help you get the most out of MIDIToTab .

For those of you who don't know what MIDIToTab is, check out the official website:

http://midi-to-tab.com

There are many features in MIDIToTab which allow to simplify and fine-tune the process of tablature optimization, but by far the most powerful one is Deep Search - the in-depth calculating mechanism of MIDIToTab which uses the latest artificial intelligence techniques to find the best possible fretting positions on the instrument.

MIDIToTab can transcribe to over 20 instruments. Here are some which cannot be found in any other tablature program:
  • 8-, 9- and 10-string guitars,
  • the lute (6 to 10 courses)
  • the bouzouki (3 to 4 strings)
  • the octave mandolin,
  • other instruments from the mandolin family: the mandocello, the mandobass and the mandola.

Tablatures can be exported in a variety of formats, including PDF, PowerTab and many more.

Be sure to check back from time to time for updates, and if you have any questions feel free to send them to help@miditotab.com .
