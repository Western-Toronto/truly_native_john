Fasting Diet Review

September 13, 2013 by Gregg Ghelfi Comments are off

Fasting Diet Review

If people were told that they could lose weight by sleeping with their pet rock, they would try it. Fasting used to be a sign of famine or religious cleansing. Nowadays, people still fast because of famine, conflict, poverty or religious observation, but some fast to lose weight.

What is the Fasting Diet?

Fasting involves abstaining from food and drink for some period, usually twenty-four hours or more. (If you consider a three-hour gap between meals a fast, you probably dont want to try a fasting diet.) Most fasting diets do not actually involve fasting but adhering to a very low-calorie for a day or two followed by a few days of eating your normal diet. Theoretically, you will lose weight and the weight loss will come from stored fat.

One type of fasting diet is called intermittent fasting. While there are several variations of this, a popular one is called the 5:2 intermittent fasting diet . You eat normally for four or five days and then you eat a very low-calorie diet for two days 500 calories for women and 600 calories for men. You can eat your calories in one or several meals and you can drink as much water as you like.

Does the fasting diet work?

Not eating is a great way to lose weight. Prison protestors have done it for hundreds of years. They may have ulterior motives, but rapid weight loss is a direct result of their fasting. Of course, fasting diets are not the abstention of food for days and months but eating and drinking far fewer calories over a couple of days than you need to survive long-term.

While you may lose weight, is it healthy?

Some research suggests that intermittent fasting improves your health. It may improve insulin sensitivity and lower stress. Other research suggests that it can prolong life by delaying the start of diseases such as heart disease and diabetes. It is possible that the fasting is affecting the participants health. It could also be that the participants are paying attention to their foods and portions on their off days and that change is positively affecting their health.

Should You Try It?

If you are relatively healthy, fasting from time to time will not likely harm you. Just remember to drink plenty of fluids and eat nutritional foods during your fasting days. You still need the usual amounts of vitamins, minerals and other nutrients even if you are drastically reducing your calorie intake, so take a multivitamin. Keep in mind, once you stop fasting, your body adjusts and you may even be hungrier. This is the stage where most dieters go back to their old bingeing habits and regain the weight they lost.

Fasting diets, if you choose to follow one, should be under medical supervision because prolonged fasting can lead to fatigue, constipation, dehydration, dizziness and other symptoms of malnutrition. You can even die if you arent careful. So proceed with caution and remember that better health is the goal.
