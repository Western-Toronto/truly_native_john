Arc protection clothing: new OSHA standards

Posted on December 1, 2014 by FeurySafety

Arc protection clothing to get new OSHA standards

Our friends at Bulwark University are on top of it all. They alerted us right away about the new 29 CFR 1910.269 regulation. It is a massive document which is continuallybeing studied and interpreted by the marketplace.Often referred to as OSHA 1910.269, it is the federal regulation (law) and defines the safety practices of employees working in the
generation, transmission and distribution of electricity

OSHAspreviousrules for the construction of transmission anddistribution installations are now out of date and inconsistentwith more recent general industry standards covering the operation andmaintenance of electric power generation, transmission, and distribution lines and equipment.

The new language mandates the use of flame resistant/arc-rated clothing by all electrical workers who could be exposed to an electric arc while on the job. It also presents specific deadlines for the completion of hazard assessments and the issuance of proper PPE (Personal Protection Equipment, or arc protection clothing).

OSHA announced the long awaited final rule revising the standards for power generation, transmission and distribution inApril of this year. The new provisions for protection from electric arcs include newrequirements for the employer:

  • assess the workplace toidentify employees exposed to hazards from flames or fromelectric arcs,
  • make reasonable estimates of the incident heatenergy to which the employee would be exposed,
  • ensure that theouter layer of clothing worn by employees is flame resistantunder certain conditions,
  • generally ensure that employeesexposed to hazards from electric arcs wear protective clothingand other protective equipment with an arc rating greater than orequal to the estimated heat energy.

Additionally, the rule requires that arc-rated protection (arc protection clothing) should cover the employees entire body, with limited exceptions forthe hands. feet, face and head. Arc protection clothing is not necessary for employees hands when:

  • the employee is wearing rubber insulating gloves with protectors
  • the estimated heat energy does not exceed 14/cal/cm2
  • when the employee is wearing heavy duty work gloves with a weight of at least 401 gm/m2 (12oz/yd12)

Arc protection clothing

According to ISHN ,employers will need toprovide arc protectionclothing and related PPE for arc-flash protection by April 1, 2015: head-to-toe FR clothing for all hazards greater than 2 calories or for work above 600 volts. This mandate for full body protection means that workers will now be required to wear FR pants, balaclavas or face shields, boots, and gloves in addition to FR shirts and jackets already commonly provided.

The American Society for Testing and Materials (ASTM) has adopted standards related to electric power generation, transmission, and distribution work. ASTM Committee F18 on Electrical Protective Equipment for Workers has developed standards on rubber insulating equipment, climbing equipment, protective grounding equipment, fiberglass rod and tube used in live-line tools, and clothing for workers exposed to electric arcs.
