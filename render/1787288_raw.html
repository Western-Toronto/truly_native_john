Cuba: A Destination for Travelers Looking For More than Just a Vacation


Cuba: A Destination for Travelers Looking For More than Just a Vacation


By Lauren Elizondo
May 28, 2013

As a Caribbean island, Cuba has everything youd expect: tropical terrain, stretches of white sand beaches and delectable coastal cuisine. But to reduce this beautifully intricate country to a common island destination would be doing international travelers and Cubans a disservice, to say the least.

While its true that until very recently, Americans could not legally travel to Cuba, the U.S. Treasury Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) is now allowing U.S. citizens to travel to the island for immersion vacations, via People-to-People licenses. What does that mean for you? Travel companies, such as Friendly Planet Travel, have created Cuban tours that allow travelers to fully immerse themselves in Cuban culture through programs involving art, education, agriculture, etc. Cuba may not be the right destination for all travelers, but it could be right for you if you agree with these reasons to visit:

Shutterstock
You Want to Learn about what its Like to Live in Cuba | Although it may be easy to watch a documentary about a country and assume you know what it is like to live there, its still no substitute for actually experiencing the customs and land in person. Through the one-on-one cultural exchange program Cuba offers travelers, it is not only possible to learn about life in Cuba while vacationing there, it is the sole reason for your trip. For instance, you can learn about education on this Caribbean island by visiting a school and spending time with children in their classroom. Meeting face-to-face with Cuban entrepreneurs, artists, farmers and other citizens will give you an in-depth understanding of life in Cuba not the cliff-notes vacation version that many Caribbean destinations provide.

You Want to See and Understand Cubas Natural Beauty | Itdoesn'ttake a rocket scientist to understand why Caribbean islands are warmer than say, Iceland. But unless you do extensive research about the ecosystem and agriculture present on the island you are traveling to, chances are you will go there and never really know why the land is the way it is. Traveling to Cuba with the People-to-People license, on the other hand, opens doors to understanding the land and the history behind it from those who know it best Cuba citizens. Visit a Cuban cigar farm in the scenic Pinar del Rio Province countryside and learn about why the terrain is ideal to grow tobacco, as well as how farmers in the area came together during the Cuban Revolution and overcame their hardships.

Shutterstock
You Want Your Trip to be Meaningful | Whether you are a first-time traveler or a seasoned tourist, its likely you want your vacation to be meaningful, or at least memorable. And if you are looking for more than a beach to sunbathe on, Cuba is an excellent choice. There is no better way to immerse yourself in a culture than by spending time on a daily basis with residents. The idea that Cuban people dont like Americans is a common misconception that has kept travelers away from this whole other world a mere 230 miles from U.S. shores. In reality, the embargo has deeply affected Cubans, but has not damaged the fondness they hold for Americans. This is apparent in the welcoming communities on the island. Youll likely come to find that your curiosity about Cuban customs is very similar to those of Cuban citizens regarding life off the island.
