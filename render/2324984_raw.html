The Donald Sterling Decision and how the NBA just Tricked the Slaves into Thinking They are Free

Editorial
April 30, 2014
Cosmo
April 30, 2014

Related Items adam silver NBA racist sterling

While African Americans and media outlets are celebrating the punishment handed out to Donald Sterling what we are overlooking are the rights that were taken from him and the precedent that this decision is setting.

To be clear Sterling has received a lifetime ban, $2.5 million fine and he will be forced to sell the team. Clippers president Andy Roeser will handle day to day operations of the club.

THE PRIVACY OF HIS OWN HOME

This man was having a conversation in the privacy of his own home.

People are drawing comparisons to employees getting fired regarding comments posted on Facebook and twitter. Sterling was not publicly expressing his views on some media outlet. He was simply talking to his significant other about how he wants his business conducted. These views right or wrong has earned this man $1.9 billion dollars. Sterling is self-made and has conducted business a certain way that has made him extremely successful. In the privacy of his own home he was advising how he wanted her to conduct herself and why.

Other comparisons mention the NBAs code of conduct again, not the same thing. He was not breaking any laws and commentary was in the privacy of his own house. Trust me, if you put audio in the leagues house you would hear more than your share of politically incorrect commentary.

LETS BE HONEST

98% of us have made some type of racist stereotypical remark in the past, although privately. The Black People can Dance joke, the Asian men have small penises joke, the Jewish people are cheap joke. I could probably sit here and rattle off at least 50 or so that Ive heard from different people in different PRIVATE conversations. These are comments that you would never, ever make out in public. Im guilty of it. I have conversations every day, that if they went public could ruin my career thats why they are private conversations. Granted, they are not racists, but many time they are accusational in nature calling others racist. Something that I would never say publicly if I wanted to stay employed.

IDEALLY WHAT SHOULD HAVE HAPPENED

In my opinion this was not for the NBA to manage. This should have been managed via social justice. This was something that needed to be managed by the people. If people simply stopped going to the games, the franchise would not be profitable and Sterling would be forced to sell. This is how it should work. There is freedom of speech but it comes with social penalties not professional penalties. When we start handing out institutionalized penalties as a result of a breach in privacy, we began to encroach one of our biggest most important freedoms.

NBA Commissioner Adam Silver

THE NBA IS A BUSINESS

The NBA is smart. They were aware of the financial impacts and knew that something had to be done immediately. I guarantee that Donald Sterling is not the only owner who feels the way he does, but he was thrown to the wolves. He was the sacrificial lamb. Him getting caught jeopardized everyones money from the coaches, owners and players down to the parking lot attendants and concession stand workers. If there was a walkout then no one would make any money. If other players joined in, the entire league would get shut down and we all know that it takes years for a major league to comeback from any type of strike. Not to mention, we are in the middle of the Playoffs!!

Hundreds of millions of dollars would have been lost.

WHAT THE NBA SHOULD HAVE DONE

The NBA and other team owners should have met with Sterling quickly and privately and asked him to resign. Asked him to submit a written and formal apology and as parts of that apology state that he made the decision to put the franchise up for sale, and asked him to make a donation to an anti-discrimination charity.

Believe it or not, Sterling is the victim. He has been victimized by his side piece and the NBA. They are referring to Stiviano as the Sidepiece Civil Rights Superhero! Really. She is a money digging ho who violated this man after he has given her more money than she could earn for herself in 10 lifetimes.

To be clear, I dont feel sorry for him, he has lived a great life and will continue to live a great life. This to shall pass for Mr. Sterling. He is worth $1.9 billion dollars so he can by a small country, kick everyone out and live every second of his last years in peace and quiet. The statements he said were disgusting, sad, ignorant and racist, however he is still the victim.

MY FEAR.

Until all races are represented on all levels of society we will have these closet racists.and they have the money. My fear is that similar to when we elected Obama that people thought racism would be dead. In some ways, having a black president made things worse.

My fear is that we think that the playing field is finally even and with that thought we stop trying to improve, leaving things truly uneven thus allowing the divide to continue to exist.

My fear is that the NBA has pulled the wool over everyones eyes including the players. This is just the rest of the Donald Sterlings (league owners) tricking the players into thinking they are standing by their side, keeping them happy so they can continue to play basketball in essence, they are just tricking the slaves into thinking they are free.
