5 Patterned Dress Shirts to Try Instead of Solids

January 12, 2015 4:06 pm Published by Pierre Mohan 2 Comments

Feel like all you wear is solid-color shirts? Patterns are an easy way to showcase your style. For formal occasions, youll probably wear solids like white, black, and navy. But for a less-formal event, the right pattern can take your look from just OK to trendsetting. Be that guy who pushes the boundaries have fun with what you wear! Below, see five patterns from classic to modern thatll look great with your custom suit .

Photos from Honeybuy & Everlane

1. Stripes

In 3 words: classic, simple, and elegant

Stripes are a great way to break away from pure solids. Its uncertain who was the first to wear stripes. In 1846, Queen Victoria dressed her young son in sailor stripes . This set a fashion trend for the children of England. Coco Chanel was the first fashion designer to introduce stripes by adding them to her nautical collection of 1917.

Today, modern striped shirts are composed of very thin stripes (see above). Try finding a thinly striped shirt in a color you already wear for an easy transition.

Photos from Everlane & Macys

2. Gingham

In 3 words: timeless, rugged, and dapper

This medium-weight pattern originated in Manchester, England during the mid-18th century. It is still wildly popular today. Gingham is a checkered pattern that comes in nearly any color combination. Look for a gingham shirt composed of two colors you like to work into your wardrobe.

Photos from Barneys & Flickr

3. Plaid

In 3 words: trendy, comfortable, and easy

You may hear this pattern also called tartan . In Scotland during the 17th century, warrior clans represented themselves by wearing their own combination of tartan colors similar to countries and sports teams today. The pattern became widespread in Europe after King George IVs visit to Scotland in 1822.

Fast forward to today. Every fall, plaid revives itself in cozy flannel material. Its not just for lumberjacks; you will also see it in dress shirt material. Use it as a classy way to add a little spunk to your formal looks. Find a plaid color combination you like and go for it!

Photos from BlueFly , Hitched , & Lookastic

4. Botanicals & Florals

In 3 words: bold, colorful, and beautiful

Flowers and other rare botanicals have always been sought after by humankind ever heard of the Dutch tulip mania of the 1630s? At its peak, a single tulip bulb sold for more than 10 times the yearly salary of a skilled craftsman.

Youve probably seen your grandma wearing floral patterns, but they have made a major fashion comeback over the last three years. From skate shops to Gucci, many major designers and men have embraced this menswear trend. To get started with this trend, look for two-color patterns like the small-flowered blue button-up pictured above.

Photos from Lyst , Lyst , & SeenHeardKnown

5. Abstract/Graphic Prints

In 3 words: futuristic, artsy, and fun

Many designers release innovative patterns each year. A bold pattern makes a statement, but there are also more subtle patterns to ease you into this trend. Look for abstract, graphic, marbled, dip-dyed, and ombre long-sleeved dress shirts. You can even buy a basic cotton button-down and try to make your own creation with Rit dye .
