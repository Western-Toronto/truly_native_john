Increasing Conversions 276% for $577 in Paid Advertising

Weve spoken about the importance of having really intriguing content to build credibility for your business . Now that you have this content, how do you focus on getting it in front of the right people? Nothing is free, you have to spend money to make money. Lets talk about how.

There are quite a few options to choose from. Google AdWords, Facebook, Twitter, Linkedin, StumbleUpon, Reddit, and more. My best advice would be to focus on one paid channel at a time and become comfortable with the service. Add new channels as you can handle and afford, get rid of ones that arent performing.

The most important piece to paid advertising is measurement. If you cant measure your conversions or goals on a particular paid channel, you shouldnt be spending money on it. If a paid channel is not performing after a fair amount of time, there is no harm in ditching it. Dont worry if your site traffic fades. Business is about conversions, not site visits. Visits can lead to conversions, but your budget is small and you need channels that are measurable.

There are two types of content that youll promote. One will be about your service, the other about your industry and knowledge of it. Each type requires a different approach. Ill walk through what weve been doing over the past month that has grown our trials by 276% and our Facebook likes 181% for a total of $577.53 in paid advertising.

At this early stage, our main goal is to get people in the door and increase trial signups. To accomplish this, we directed people to our home page or to specific blog posts. All the following numbers are visits to pages, with no measurement of conversions by channel. That is something we arent measuring yet, but will begin to in March. This first month has been about experimentation.

Clicks for Free

In the first few days, I spent no money. Instead, I pounded the digital pavement looking for people that were looking for us. I searched for blog posts that discussed alternatives to our main competitors, then made well-thought out comments. I used that strategy again on a question/answer site called Quora. Lastly, I made my way to Twitter and made direct suggestions to people asking for advice on CRM or sales software. Going out and proactively promoting our product without being too overbearing turned out to be quite helpful and something I will continue to do from time to time.

Here are visits for the month from my unpaid marketing strategies to our Home Page:

Facebook

Now that I had some free breadcrumbs working for us, I wanted to see what Facebook had to offer. I was pleasantly surprised at how far the advertising platform has come since I used it years ago. I took our Visual Pipeline graphic from our home page and made a simple ad then narrowed to my desired demographic. This is where Facebook shines, if im looking for people who like small business software, I can make that blanket statement as shown here:

In a few clicks, I can have my ad displayed for Men and Women who live in the United States between the ages of 25 and 45 who are interested in small business software. No other ad platform that Im aware of can get that granular with such ease.

Since ad campaigns were so easy to set up, I created several. One ongoing campaign for $10 a day driving clicks to our homepage then a small $10-$20 campaign per blog post I created.

Google AdWords

Google is the web advertising behemoth, but its a difficult platform to use. It also requires a lot of research on what keywords or phrases people are searching for and the cost of those clicks. What are people searching for when they are looking for your business? It might not be as straightforward as you think, and if it is, then the cost per click for your desired keywords are likely quite expensive. In our case, our competitors are spending $30 per click to drive traffic from Google to their website. Want to know why Salesforce charges $65-$125 per user per month? Other than their extravagant parties, they enjoy spending money like drunken sailors on marketing.

I dont have the budget to compete with Salesforce, so I got a little lucky and a little clever. Turns out a competitor of ours is selling their CRM product to focus on their Project Management software business. In any sale like this, customers get overlooked. I found that roughly 200 people a month search for our competitor and there were little to no advertisers for them as a keyphrase. I whipped up a quick landing page and set up a Google AdWords campaign. At $3.62 per click, this is more than twice as expensive than Facebook, but is a much more focused audience.

StumbleUpon Paid Discovery

StumbleUpon is Pandora for websites. If you want some fresh random stuff, just StumbleUpon then thumbs up/down the sites they provide. They will show you more and more sites that are in your preferred interest buckets. After you StumbleUpon enough, the software gets better at showing you content you want. A great suggestion from Alex Turnbell on Grooves blog recommended StumbleUpon Paid Discovery.

At first, I used this on our homepage to dismal results. We had a ton of hits for $0.17 each, but were staying on the site for roughly a second before moving on. Once I re-read the post, I realized StumbleUpon would be best for blog content. As soon as I made that switch, StumbleUpon became a fantastic source of traffic. Visitors only averaged eight seconds per visit, but thats not bad considering visitors are coming to our site blind.

TL;DR

All said and done, our collective efforts cost us $577.53 this month and increased our trials from 38 to 105 for a 276% increase. Our next step is to measure which of these channels(if any) is providing more conversions than another. Well also begin to measure what percentage of these trials are converting into paying customers.

Next week, well be writing about the services we use to measure our inbound web traffic then how we nurture them into paying customers.
