In-Cider Information (infographic)

Posted on Wednesday May 14, 2014

Interesting results from our cider research:

  • In the 18-24 age range, cider is even more popular with women.
  • Cider made from pears is called "Perry"
  • 45% of apples grown in the UK are ised for cider
  • The method of making champagne was first used for cider in England
  • Children in the 14th century were baptised in cider
  • Captain Cook carried cider on his ships to treat his crew for scurvy
  • Cider making has become so popular in Tassie that in 2012 they failed to export a single apple for the first time in 130 years
  • William Bligh planted the first Australian apple trees in Tasmania. Today, Tassie is known as the apple isle.

Australian Liquor Marketers Pty Ltd (ALM) and Cellarbrations support the responsible service and consumption of alcohol.
Liquor Act 2010 (ACT): it is an offence to supply alcohol to a person under the age of 18, penalties apply.
Liquor Control Reform Act 1998 (Vic): WARNING: It is an offence to supply alcohol to a person under the age of 18 years (Penalty exceeds $7,000), for a person under the age of 18 years to purchase or receive liquor (Penalty exceeds $600).
Liquor Control Act 1988 (WA): WARNING. Under the Liquor Control Act 1988, it is an offence: to sell or supply liquor to a person under the age of 18 years on licenced or regulated premises; or for a person under the age of 18 years to purchase or attempt to purchase, liquor on licensed or regulated premises. ALM supplies retailer customers under wholesale licence number 6160007450, 218 Bannister Road, Canning Vale, WA 6155.
Liquor Act 2007 (NSW): It is against the law to sell or supply alcohol to, or to obtain alcohol on behalf of, a person under the age of 18 years.
Liquor Act 1979 (NT): a person must not sell or otherwise supply liquor to a child (being a person under the age of 18).
Liquor Act 1992 (QLD): Under the act it is an offence to supply liquor to a person under the age of 18 years.
Liquor Licensing Act 1997 (SA): it is an offence to sell or supply liquor to a minor.
Liquor Licensing Act 1990 (Tas): liquor must not be sold or supplied to a person under the age of 18.
For more specific legislation in your state or territory visit our Liquor Licensing Acts page .
