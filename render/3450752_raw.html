Creating Logos in Illustrator Workshop April 4

by Scott Kaneshiro
in classes

Motive
Logos are a core element in creating a brand identity, yet they can sometimes be a pain to create. While some may choose Photoshop as their design tool of choice, top graphic designers will use Illustrator instead. Unlike Photoshop, Illustrator is vector-based and enables users much more power to create sharp, clean, and scalable graphics. However, while many users are familiar with the pixel-based Photoshop, many do not feel comfortable quite yet with the vector-based tools in Illustrator.

Course Description
In this workshop, students will be recreating 2 logos with an instructor in hands-on activities, discussing tips and tricks in understanding good logo design. To partake in the activities, students will need to bring in their own laptop and own a copy of Illustrator CS4 or higher. The instruction will have step-by-step demonstrations of each Illustrator effect. The last half hour of class will be dedicated to answering questions or starting on personal projects with the instructors help.

For Future Study
We highly recommend that students continue to take the Illustrator Fundamentals class afterwards. In that class, students will be able to break down each feature, dive deeper into each concept, and learn ways to put all of them together to apply in real life scenarios.

  • Understanding the timeline and keyframes
  • Adjusting length of video clips
  • Applying transitions

  • Placing B roll
  • Adding a soundtrack and adjusting sound levels

4 Steps to Become a Google Certified Partner

by Anita Wu
in General , Google

We are now aGoogle Certified Partner! In addition to providing great training, we have a Google Partner Profile to show that we can be trusted to provide great advertising on Google as well.

AGoogle Partner meets at least 4 different requirements. Some are harderthan others, but here is a quick run down to get you started.

1. Complete your Partners company profile

The easieststep! Just visit https://www.google.com/partners and select Im an Agency in the top right corner. Having an existingGoogle account makes this process much simpler.

Its important to note itneeds to be affiliated with aMy Client Center (MCC) manager account for you to become a Google Partner.

2.Get certified in AdWords

The certification shows that you have mastered AdWords in one form or another. To be certified, you must pass the AdWords Fundamentals exam in addition to one of the following exams: Advanced Search (which our CEO took to get certified!), Advanced Display, or Shopping. Each test allots 120 minutes, meaning you have a little over a minute for each question.

Creating a Partners profilegives you easy access totheir study materials and they are quite comprehensive. You can check out their YouTube channel for Refresher Hangouts and other tidbits.

3. Meet the Spend requirement

If you are already actively using AdWords, then this step shouldnt be too much of a barrier. Your managed accounts need to show that your agency has a healthy amount of activity. This means having:

  1. At least$10,000 (or local currency equivalent) spent over the last 90 days.
  2. Spend activity in at least 2/3 of the last 90 days.
  3. An MCC manager account is at least 90 days old.

4. Implement the recommended best practices

Your client accounts must show that youre maximizing their AdWords performance. Google willreview your client accounts starting with when it was associated with your MCC manager account. They lay out examples of what you should be doing, but the point is that you should be actively adjusting the campaigns and utilizing most of their tools and features.

We hope this helps a few of you get started on your path to becoming a Google Partner. Let us know if this helped or if you have any questions by visiting our Facebook or tweeting @academyx !
