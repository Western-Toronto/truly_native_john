Top 6 Reasons We Love Meredith Vieira

  • By Jennifer Laskey
View All
    • of
  • NBCUniversal/The Meredith Vieira Show

    Cheers to Meredith Vieira!

    Meredith Vieira is a popular television host, Emmy Award-winning journalist, and Everyday Health partner and contributor but shes no diva. Down-to-earth and funny, she demonstrates her devotionto her family, compassion and empathy for others, and her commitment to making a difference in peoples lives all in the public eye. Best of all, she's never afraid to show her emotions, even if that means getting teary-eyed while hosting her new daytime television show. Here are more reasons we cant get enough of her.

  • Jim Wright/Corbis Outline

    Shes a Voice for Conditions Like MS and Alzheimer's

    Meredith doesn't just talk about her passion for health issues. Shes packed The Meredith Vieira Show with an inspiring lineup of health stories, and has spoken openly in interviews and on her previous shows (the Today show and The View) about her own familys medical issues. Every family has its own struggles, she wrote in her debut Everyday Health blog . My husband, Richard [Cohen] , was diagnosed with multiple sclerosis when he was 25. And he's had two bouts of colon cancer. Yet, if you were to walk into our home on any given day, the sound you'd most likely hear is laughter. We've found it's the best medicine of all. Meredith has also seen firsthand the devastating effects of Alzheimers disease, having lost her father, an older brother, and an aunt to the illness.

  • Splash News/Corbis

    She Is a Model of Good Health

    Meredith takes care of herself, which is something everybody should do more of. You're no good to others unless you're good to yourself. I never pass up dark green leafy vegetables, although I wish they came on a cone with sprinkles! she says. She also tries to walk or hike every day. Nothing nourishes my soul more than the smell of an ocean breeze or the crunch of pine needles under my hiking boots. I spend most of my free time walking along the Hudson River near my home.

  • NBCUniversal/The Meredith Vieira Show

    She Found Real Balance

    While Meredith has had an extremely successful and demanding career, she has always insisted on a healthy work-life balance. It hasn't always been easy. She quit her job at 60 Minutes after the birth of her second child, in 1991. And after five years as co-host of the Today show, she left in 2011 to spend more time with her family. With the launch of The Meredith Vieira Show, she has finally achieved the right balance, personally and professionally.

  • NBCUniversal/The Meredith Vieira Show

    She Rallies for Patients

    Having experienced her familys health challenges up-close, Meredith uses her new show to put a spotlight on people living with, and overcoming, difficulties. She launched The Meredith Vieira Show with a segment highlighting Dancing Dreams , a program that helps kids with serious physical challenges turn their dreams of dancing into reality. An early show focused on Alzheimers disease, with Seth Rogen and his wife, Lauren Miller whose mom has Alzheimers and a young coupledealing with early onset Alzheimers. Other segments have featured former Olympic swimmer Amy Van Dyken-Rouen (above), who was paralyzed from the waist down in an ATV accident earlier this year, and a young boy who contracted swine flu at age 5, subsequently underwent 77 surgeries, and is now a healthy 10-year-old. All we really want is to feel connected, she says. Remember the last time someone said something nice to you and turned a bad day into a beautiful one? Well, pay it forward. Spreading kindness is contagious.

  • Christopher Polk/NBC/Getty Images

    She's Just Plain Honest

    Whether discussing her husband's medical issues , her personal struggles, or simply sharing the things that make Meredith Meredith, she's the real deal. On the debut of her new show, the audience learned that Meredith curses like a sailor; likes a tall glass of wine"; and is really bad at driving, putting dishes in the dish rack, and singing (even though she loves to sing). "She's real ... real crazy," Cohen cracked on her first show.

  • Everett Collection/Alamy

    She Has a Sense of Humor

    Meredith has summed up her sense of humor this way: I enjoy laughing so hard I cry. I hate laughing so hard I pee. This is a woman who believes in facing lifes ups and downs with a lot of laughter . She recently took a pie to the face for the Pies 4 Paws Challenge , and we cant wait for more comedic segments like Car-eoke on her new show!
