The Top Five Most Surprising Grammy Nominations

December 10, 2013 10:34 PM
Tweet
  • Subscribe to RSS
  • Subscribe to Mail

Written by Sara Magnola Taner

The 2014 Grammy nominations , announced this past weekend, have many people scratching their heads in confusion. The expected big names Jay Z, Justin Timberlake, Lorde, Taylor Swift, and Kendrick Lamar all got nods but how they were (and in some cases werent) nominated is causing some surprise.

Top 5 Surprise Nominations for Grammy 2014

1) While hip-hop heavy hitter Jay-Z earned nine nominations the most nods accumulated by any artist this year none of them fell within the most prestigious top three categories: Record of the Year, Album of the Year, and Song of the Year.

2) Already decorated fan-favorite Justin Timberlake also picked up an impressive seven total nominations, including Best Pop Solo Performance for Mirrors and Best Pop Duo/Group Performance with Jay Z for Suit & Tie. However none of his nominations fell within the big three either.

PICS:Justin Timberlake & Jay-Z goof around on the set of 'Suit & Tie'

3) Newcomer Lorde who topped the Billboard Hot 100 in 2013 with the release of her first single Royals is probably not caught up in a love affair with being overlooked for a Best New Artist nomination. Ironically, she did get nominations for two of the big three: Record of the Year and Song of the Year.

4) In the almost antiquated category of Album of the Year the nomination given to Sara Bareilles for her fourth album The Blessed Unrest, stood out like a sore thumb among the fresh likes of Taylor Swifts Red, Kendrick Lamars Good Kid, M.A.A.D City, and Macklemore & Ryan Lewis The Heist.

5) While she has mostly made headlines for her ridiculous twerking antics lately, Miley Cyrus hit Wrecking Ball has been a hit in its own right this year, so it came as a surprise that she didnt get acknowledged.
