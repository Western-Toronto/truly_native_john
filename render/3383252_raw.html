Flying with Plastic

Environmentally Fucked

Trevor McGill

Albatrosses, in particular Laysan Albatrosses can go years flying without touching land, their wings span more than 6 feet and they adorably stick with a single mate for life, but plastic is killing them and we humans are responsible.

That plastic bag you got from the supermarket yesterday, that you used for 5 minutes getting to the car, then from car park to kitchen bench will take a 1000 years to break down in the environment.

5.25 trillion pieces of plastic debris are in the Ocean, off that mass 269,000 tons float on the surface. 4 billion plastic mirco fibers per square kilometer litter the deep sea.

Every year shoppers use approximately 500 billion single use plastic bags, which translates to a million bags every minute across the year or 150 bags a year per person and that number is on the increase.

The plastic albatrosses and other birds are eating is dumped in the ocean and photographic evidence from Chris Jordan from the Midway Atoll, in the Northwestern Hawaiian Islands tells a cruel tale of birds decomposing carcasses filled with plastic they eat mistakenly for surface fish and squid. Feeling full the chicks in particular dont go in search of other food and sometimes die from malnutrition. Its leading to 40% of Laysan Albatross chicks dying before they hit the fledgling stage.

Some communities have taken the banning of plastic seriously Bangladesh did so in 2002, but the initiative wasnt inspired by helping bird life, rather a damaging typhoon alerted authorities to the millions of bags that clogged the systems flood drains and contributed massively to the destruction that occurred.

France banned all non-biodegradable plastic bags in 2010, the Italians followed suit, the Chinese prohibit bags less than 0.025 mm thick but this is totally ineffective in terms of their population and level of pollution occurring there. The Irish imposed a tax on plastics and recorded a 90% drop in the use of plastic as a result the tax money went to a recycling program. Even in Taiwan you cant get a plastic bag without paying for it. Australia has called for a voluntary ban and thus far theyre claiming consumption has fallen by 90% but were skeptical given the amount of bags were offered in our day to day business.

The situation in North America is dire. Northern Fulmar birds, that feed exclusively at sea, researchers are finding have plastic in their stomach for long periods and as a result the birds are washing up on the coasts of Washington, Oregon, British Columbia and Canada. The scientists attribute their deaths to the increase in plastic pollution over the past four decades. 90% of the Fulmar's found dead had ingested plastics including twine, Styrofoam, candy wrappers and on average 36.8 pieces of plastic was found in each bird. Imagine if we humans were forced into orally consuming that much plastic?

The Monterey Bay Aquarium waded into the discussion saying globally, up to 1 million seabirds and 100,000 marine mammals and sea turtles die each year from eating plastic.

Plastic pellets operate like magnets for toxic chemicals like DDT and PCBs and effectively become poison pills and Japanese researchers found that concentrations of these chemicals were as much as a million times higher than in the water. Plastics themselves leach endocrine-disrupting chemicals like biphenyl into the ocean.

Whales being autopsied according to a British Marine Biologist are revealing stomachs full of plastic. Whale species like the Beaked Whale who feed of squid are particularly prone with plastic bags strongly resembling squid submerged. Then of course whales are susceptible to swallowing plastic bags due to the circumference of their mouths and the intake of water they imbibe.

In Australia a 10 foot tagged wildlife tracked crocodile turned up dead at Magnetic Island after consuming 25 plastic shopping bags.

Conservationist estimate at least 100,000 mammals and birds die annually from the 500 billion + plastic bags produced and used around the world. The fish killed is unable to be verified but they assume that statistic to run into the millions!

Stephanie Avery-Gomm a graduate student in University of British Columbia's Department of Zoology revealed that these birds "stomach content provides a 'snapshot' sample of plastic pollution from a large area of the northern Pacific Ocean [and] despite the close proximity of the 'Great Pacific Garbage Patch'an area of concentrated plastic pollution in the middle of the North Pacific gyre, plastic pollution has not been considered an issue of concern off our coast," Avery-Gomm said. "But we've found similar amounts and incident rates of plastic in beached northern fulmars here as those in the North Sea. This indicates it is an issue which warrants further study."

Debris affects over 44% of seabird species worldwide through entanglement and ingestion (Laist 1997). Over 13,000 pieces of plastic float on every square km of ocean, these floating pollutants travel in currents to even the remotest locations and although waste disposal in the marine environment is managed via international conventions and agreements we believe not enough is being done in terms of reducing plastic production, seeing to its recycling nor ensuring it is not being systematically dumped in our oceans.

This is one of the greatest man made disasters happening and too few of us are making a stand. Write to your government minister today complaining about the over consumption of plastic in your community.

Lets start doing something now. And dont take another plastic bag ever or youre culpable too!
