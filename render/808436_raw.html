Los Angeles Driving Tips


Are you a driver in Los Angeles? Given that you need a car to get anywhere in LA, chances are that you drive often. RelayRides blogger Brad Iger runs through some of the best driving tips for the City of Angels.

Moving to the City of Angels, or just stopping by? Either way, youd be wise to arm yourself with some street-smart insight on how to survive the urban jungle of LA. If you want to get to just about anything in Los Angeles , youre going to be spending some time on the roads of this sprawling metropolis, so youd be wise to properly prepare yourself for the journey ahead.

The Freeways Are Faster

No matter how bad the 101 may look, nine times out of ten, its still a better bet than the surrounding surface streets. Rather than putting yourself and your ride through the undue stress of jam packed surface streets filled with stoplights, pedestrians, cyclists, unprotected turns and poorly executed street parking performances, sometimes its better to just accept your fate and deal with the freeway crawl.

Give Yourself An Hour

Los Angeles is big. Really big . As such, traffic issues occurring on the other side of town often can have a domino effect on all the other arteries of the city. So whether its 8 in the morning or 7 at night, and youre headed from Santa Monica to Downtown or from the Valley to Pasadena, err on the side of caution and give yourself at least an houryoull probably need it.

Build Good Karma

As tempting as it may be at times to cut into a row of stopped cars at the last minute, tailgate a slow-mover, or skip using a turn signaldont. Not only are these the habits that create traffic, theyre habits that establish the norm. While theres nothing wrong with driving assertively, driving aggressively and carelessly just causes more traffic and accidents, which in turn makes drivers impatient, which in turn restarts the cycle. Break the cycle, Los Angelesyour fellow Angelinos will thank you for it.

Read Parking Signs Carefully

Did you know you have to turn you front wheels in a certain direction based upon which way your car is facing when you park on certain streets in Hollywood? Or that last year, apron parking in Silverlake was banned , and then unbanned a few months later? Dont learn the hard waystudy neighborhood parking signs like theyre crib sheets for the SATs.

Take the Scenic Route

One aspect of living in Los Angeles that quickly becomes apparent after spending some time in the city is that quite often, it truly is the journey and not the destination that makes living in LA special.

Make a point to take Laurel Canyon Boulevard from Hollywood to Studio City, and Sunset Blvd from Silverlake all the way west as it twists through Brentwood and ends just south of Malibu at Pacific Coast Highway. And if you love to drive on winding mountain roads, Mulholland Drive and Angeles Crest Highway provide some of the most incredible driving roads on the West Coast, and are sure to satiate the appetite of the most discerning corner-carvers out there.
