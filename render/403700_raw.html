5 Ways To Improve Results Before Listing Your Home For Sale

February 3rd, 2014

February 3rd, 2014

By Bill Gassett

How can you improve your results when selling Real Estate? The answer is not all that difficult. Proper preparation is the key to selling your home for a good price and in a time frame you are comfortable with.

This is something that many sellers fail to fully consider, and it is often the lack of preparation that leaves these sellers frustrated in the home selling process. If you take care of all the prep work, the actual selling process goes much more smoothly. However, it is hard to prepare well if you do not know where to start.

Focus On Gathering Information

Many sellers do not grasp how important the information gathering process is when it comes to selling a home. Before a For Sale sign ever comes close to your lawn, you need to have all of your information ready and use that information to decide on your selling strategy. It is not just a matter of gathering info, either.

You need to understand the right kind of information to collect. Things like researching an agent, researching the market in your area and for your type of home, and ensuring you have a way to keep track of the market after your house goes up are all important. Overlooking any of these can leave you lacking once the critical marketing and selling process begins.

Create a Marketing Plan
A marketing plan is absolutely necessary if you want to successfully sell your home. This plan is something you construct with your real estate agent, and something that must take into account your individual needs and preferences. If you need to sell your home quickly, that will require one type of marketing plan.

If, on the other hand, you have the luxury of time, your plan will consist of different things. Some people try to be the best deal on the block, while others want to position themselves as the premiere home in the area for their particular price point. Each requires different marketing techniques, all of which you should determine before you put your home up for sale. Look for an agent that will think outside the box and not just do your run of the mill MLS entry and a sign in the yard. You might as well do it yourself if you go with an agent who does just enough to get by.

Realtors who will do some creative real estate marketing are worth their weight in gold. Most Real Estate agents dont want to move out of their comfort zone. You will find that unfortunately many of them do not understand you have to spend money to make money. Top producing agents understand that you have to invest back into your business.

Clearly Understand Your Homes Value

Homes can be emotionally charged possessions, but emotions can cause trouble when it comes to business deals. In the end, your home sale is a business transaction, and you need to prepare accordingly. Working with a real estate agent and/or an appraiser, you should become familiar with the true value of your home.

The true value of your home is not what you need, want or desire. Market values are determined by what other buyers were willing to pay for like properties in a given geographical radius around your property. Typically, when evaluating market value of Real Estate, the comparative data that is used should be no longer than six months old. Ideally it will be three months or less as that will give a better indicator of what homes are valued at right now. Real Estate markets can change in a heartbeat so the more recent the data the better.

Look at the data through the eyes of buyers and their agents. This is what will happen when it comes time for an offer on your home. One of the most damaging things that home sellers do is overprice their home when first listing . An overpriced home is really hard to recover from even if you reduce the home later on in the process.

Tour other homes for sale in the area and take an objective look at what you are offering buyers. This puts you in a position of confidence, as you can reliably negotiate on the price of your home. You know how much and how little you can reasonably accept and can work from there. Do keep in mind though that the best data points are homes that have sold. While knowing what your neighbor is asking for their home is important, with the blink of an eye that price can change. Dont determine the value of your own home by the inflated expectations of another seller.

Demand a Good Agent

Real estate agents are like everyone else some are amazing at what they do, others are only so-so. Keep a couple of things in mind. It is very easy to get a Real Estate license and not every Realtor does the same thing to market and sell homes.

There is only a small percentage of agents that are good at everything. Some may be great at selling properties like yours, others not so much. You need to be stringent in your choice of an agent, because he or she can make a world of difference in your selling experience.

Take the time to interview at least three different real estate agents, and bring a list of solid questions to each interview . You do not want to just go with a gut feeling here you want to know how this person will act in the day-to-day tasks of selling your home. Each agent is a little different, and you need to know that the one you pick will fit with your sale and personality.

Draft a list of interview questions pertinent to your needs. You may want to know how the agent approaches negotiations, selling problem homes, how he or she performs in valuing properties and how many homes the agent has actually sold in your town. You can come up with as many questions as you like, but make sure the agent you pick answers them satisfactorily.

If communication is important to you, one of the best ways to see if the agent will meet your expectations during the selling process is how quickly they respond to you BEFORE they have your business. Believe it or not, there are tons of agents who dont understand the importance of getting back to people right away. I know because I deal with some of these agents everyday! If you are like me, you should expect to be called or emailed back in an expeditious fashion. This is how professionals do business.

So many home owners end up being disappointed with their Realtor because they end up picking the real estate agent for all the wrong reasons . Like any other business interview you should be picking your representative based on their skills and track record for getting the job done!

Improve Your Home Now

There are a number of improvements that you can do to your home to up its value or make it sell more quickly many of which do not require a significant investment. Your agent should be able to take a look at your home and advise you on what to take out, and what you can add to make a difference in your sale. However, there are some things that are typically a good idea no matter where you live.

Removing popcorn ceilings, for instance, is a very worthwhile improvement as the majority of buyers absolutely despise them. They date a home and make it feel cheep. While this is a messy one to deal with, it can be well worth the time and effort. If you have old and worn carpets with a stain or two in them, replacement can go a long way. Not only does it drastically change the appearance of your place, the saleability will be improved immensely.

Besides carpeting, one of the cheapest home improvements with the biggest bang for your buck is painting. There is nothing that brings a home to life more than a fresh coat of paint! If you have strong colors that would not be considered neutral to most home buyers, painting becomes especially important. A good Realtor should be able to help you determine what is popular. Most of the time, your safest bet when selling is to go with soft earth tones.

Upgrading your landscaping with a few flowerbeds and a nice layer of mulch is another great way to add curb appeal, and can be done over a weekend.
Some more dramatic improvements are sometimes worth considering. If your bathroom or kitchen is seriously outdated, it may be worth it to update the cabinets or colors to make them more appealing. There are ways you can make improvements without breaking the bank with a complete renovation. For example, sometimes you can get away with just replacing cabinet doors and hardware that is in line with todays market appeal.

An even cheaper alternative if you have dark and dated cabinetry is to paint them white or a cream color. A skilled painter can totally transform a dark and drab kitchen into something worth celebrating.

Prepare Now and Benefit Later

Selling your home is time consuming and always a little stressful. However, you can do a lot to improve the process by preparing properly. Keep these five things in mind in the lead up to putting your home up for sale, and reap the rewards that preparation brings. Not only will you be more prepared, but in all likelihood, you will sell your home quicker and for more money.
