Jaeger-LeCoultre: A Timeless Rendez-Vous

Wednesday 4th September, 2013.
Combining advanced mechanics and graceful style, Jaeger-LeCoultre's new range of pieces raises the bar for women's watchmaking

In recent years there has been a decided change in ladies watches. It used to be the case that nearly all womens timepieces were smaller versions of the gents' with the addition of diamonds, yet now watchmakers are catering to the discerning female watch buyer with a host of high complications. The refined Rendez-Vous collection from Jaeger-LeCoultre aptly shows just how great this change is.

Despite being just two years old, since the Rendez-Vouss creation in 2011 it has quickly become one of the manufacturer's most sought after collections. Its simple yet intricately detailed Art Deco design was instantly iconic, while the watches themselves made an impact in more than looks. The Rendez-Vous was a collection designed entirely for women from the ground up. Rather than adapting and feminising a mens watch, the Rendez-Vous was created specifically to cater to the distinguished woman. This, combined with an array of complications, showed that the illustrious manufacturer paid heed to what was missing in the industry.

With their 180th Jubilee this year, Jaeger-LeCoultre have outdone themselves with a range of exceptional Jubilee pieces, including a technically astounding gyro tourbillon. On top of this they have added some spectacular pieces to their already remarkable Rendez-Vous range. Maintaining the line's distinctive style, the watchmaker has poured their considerable horological prowess into crafting a selection of womens high complications that perfectly balance consummate mechanics with feminine sophistication.

A number of individual models have been added to the Rendez-Vous family, each exquisite, each emanating the brands signature style and artisanal skill. The first of these is the Perpetual Calendar. A perfect blend of watchmaking tradition and elegance, the watch combines it's namesake, a perpetual calendar with indication of the entire year in four figures. The lustrous dial is subtly guilloche, adding a luxurious level of depth, while the diamond-set bezel gives it a refined jewellery look. The crown, set with an inverted diamond, allows easy adjustment of all indicators. As the watch can keep flawless track of the date until the year 2100 however, this might not be necessary.

For a simpler yet equally distinguished piece, the Rendez-Vous Night & Day is just as outstanding. Rather than guilloche, the dial is radiant mother-of-pearl, lending a silky sheen to the face. Out of this shines a gold moon, sun and stars indicating, as the name suggests, the day and night. The bold numbers are easy to read against the dial, styled like a traditiona clock for a touch of heritage. For those looking for something more complicated however, the Night & Day is also available with the most illustrious complication available, the Tourbillon. Beating mesmerizingly at six oclock, the tourbillon adds an entirely new dimension to the Rendez-Vous range. An impeccable combination of beauty and fine watchmaking, the Night & Day Tourbillon places Jaeger-LeCoultre at the forefront of womens watchmaking.

The Tourbillon makes more than one appearance throughout the new range of pieces, and in the Rendez-Vous Tourbillon Wild the prestigious complication makes its presence felt in striking fashion. The tourbillon itself sits pulsating at the bottom of the dial against a backdrop of diamonds, mother-of-pearl and feathers. An elegant blend of haute joaillerie and horology, the cubist-inspired design catches the eye and is guaranteed to draw admiring glances. Part mechanical marvel, part work of art, the Rendez-Vous Tourbillon Wild is a bold statement of intent from Jaeger-LeCoultre.

The indisputable star of the new range however has to be the magnificent Rendez-Vous Celestial. Combining the collections iconic Art Deco aesthetic with a mechanically advanced movement, the Celestial is both highly complicated and charmingly feminine, beautiful inside and out. The most impressive feature, instantly leaping out as the focal point of the watch, is the zodiac calendar. Able to display the positions of the stars at any time of year, the calendar dominates the dial entirely. With the gleaming blue of the dial this gives the impression of a night sky, enhanced by diamonds which glitter like stars themselves.

Combining high complications with the Art Deco styling that has made the brand and more particularly the Rendez-Vous so iconic, Jaeger-LeCoultre s new range of pieces sets the bar for womens watches higher than it has ever been.
