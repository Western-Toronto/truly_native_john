A Nutritional Pancreatic Insufficiency Diet

Low-Fat Foods That Can Help Manage Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency

Wondering what's best to eat if you have exocrine pancreatic insufficiency? These nutritious, low-fat foods can help you manage symptoms and get the nutrition you need.

By Elizabeth Shimer Bowers
Medically reviewed by Farrokh Sohrabi, MD
Print E-mail

1
image

To combat one of the most common symptoms of exocrine pancreatic sufficiency fatty, oily stools (steatorrhea) your doctor may suggest that you follow a low-fat diet, meaning that fat should provide less than 30 percent of your total calories. Low-fat foods help to avoid overloading the pancreas with fat, says Frank Gress, MD, chief of the division of gastroenterology and hepatology at the State University of New York Downstate Medical Center in Brooklyn. If the pancreas cant release digestive enzymes , it can cause malabsorption of fats and worsen inflammation in the pancreas.

Work closely with your doctor or nutritionist on any dietary changes. If youre malnourished or are on high doses of pancreatic enzymes as part of your treatment, you may require more fat.

The general guideline calls for low-fat foods rather than non-fat foods. If you take all the fat out, you lose essential fatty acids and miss out on fat-soluble vitamins like A, D, E, and K, says Suzanne H. Michel, RD, LDN, a dietitian at the Childrens Hospital of Philadelphia. The type of fat is also important. You dont want to get your daily fat from unhealthy saturated fat sources, like a steak and cheese fries, she says. Read on to learn more about the best low-fat foods to include in your diet.

Get Going With Whole Grains

2
image

The type of fiber found in oatmeal and whole-wheat breads can help bulk up the stool, improving steatorrhea in some people with exocrine pancreatic insufficiency . Whole-grain foods should replace their less-than-healthy refined counterparts like white breads, cookies, pastries, and pasta. Plus, whole grains are a good source of B vitamins, which can be beneficial if youre not absorbing nutrients properly, Michel says.

Stock Up on Skim Milk and Low-Fat Cheeses

3
image

Everyone needs calcium, especially people with exocrine pancreatic insufficiency. If you have steatorrhea, you arent properly absorbing either fat or minerals, including calcium. Skim milk and low-fat cheeses are an excellent source of calcium," Michel says. "As long as youre not having trouble maintaining a healthy weight, you can use low-fat instead of high-fat sources of calcium as part of a pancreatic insufficiency diet .

Eat Your Vegetables

4
image

Vegetables are important sources of vitamins and minerals, and youll generally want to eat three or more servings every day. Just skip high-fat toppings like butter and sour cream. If youre taking pancreatic enzymes and can handle one of the healthier margarines that contain olive oil or vegetable oil, they can provide a good source of essential fatty acids, Michel says. Olive oil and vegetable oils are preferred fats for cooking, and steaming and blanching are healthy cooking methods. Some people with exocrine pancreatic insufficiency may be advised to eat fewer fruits and vegetables since they contain fiber, which can cause gas and bloating. Talk to your doctor or nutritionist about how much fresh produce is right for you.

Enjoy Fruits and Desserts in Moderation

5
image

Plan on two or more servings of fruit every day from berries and melons to whole fruits like apples and bananas. Fruit makes a filling snack and a sweet finish to a meal. Fruit also makes a great dessert choice, especially since nutritionists recommend eliminating the refined sugars found in typical desserts. Fruits are good sources of both vitamins and soluble fiber, Michel says. Oranges, which are high in vitamin C and potassium, are particularly beneficial if youre on a pancreatic insufficiency diet .

Pick Up Some Lean Protein

6
image

We all need protein, and people with exocrine pancreatic insufficiency should choose low-fat protein sources to prevent heart disease, just like anyone else. Think of healthy protein choices like skinless chicken and fish. Other good sources of lean protein are tofu and beans.

Sip on Decaf Coffee or Tea

7
image

Herbal tea may provide some beneficial nutrients for people with exocrine pancreatic insufficiency , but don't consume a tremendous amount, Michel says. She adds that an occasional cup of coffee or tea is okay, especially if you skip the high-fat cream. But be careful not to go overboard. In general, water is a much better beverage choice for a pancreatic insufficiency diet than caffeinated drinks.

Stir Up a Low-Fat Soup

8
image

Although soup broths and bullions may give you a nice warm feeling in your belly, as part of a pancreatic insufficiency diet they won't add a lot to your nutrient needs, Michel says. But if you have steatorrhea , simpler broths are definitely a better option than processed soups or those made with heavy cream are. To give broth a more nutrient-packed punch, Michel recommends adding some carrots or lean protein, like chicken.
