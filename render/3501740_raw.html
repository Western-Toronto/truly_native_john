Party like the locals

Get into the heart of Belfast City in time for the big countdown

Belfast knows how to throw a party

If youre after music, dancing and the chance to dress up, head to the New Years Eve party at Belfasts most popular arts venue The MAC. With a live house band youll be sure to see out the year with a bang. Alternatively, if youre looking for a little more rock n roll, take to the Limelight for Katys Open Mic night.

Real people, a real city a real laugh

Relax and raise a glass to the new year

If youre into the food scene, then try the New Years Eve menu at Shiro the newest addition to the Linen Quarter. With a growing reputation for its unique blend of European and Asian styles, it really is the talk of the town. If Italian food is more your thing then head to Coppi in St. Annes Square. With authentic dishes and a delicious selection of cocktails, its the perfect spot to start the night.

Belfast is bustling it has something for everyone

Explore in the day

  • Test your nerve and cross the famous Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge that links the mainland to the island of Carrick-a-Rede.
  • Dont miss one of Belfasts oldest attractions, St. Georges Market. Its considered one of the best markets in the UK.
  • Need to rest your feet? The quirky Harlem Cafe is the perfect spot to unwind, grab a coffee and catch up with friends.

Party in the night

  • Celebrate in style at the first Governors Ball at Crumlin Road Gaol, with live entertainment and plenty of dancing.
  • Everyones welcome at the unique Filthy McNastys a quirky pub with a secret garden and a charming atmosphere.
  • Ring in the New Year at Cabaret Supper Club for Midnight in Paris an extravagant circus themed show.
Breathe in the new year

Belfast has so much to offer, so take a step into the great outdoors and get ready to explore

Start the year as you mean to go on in Belfast City

One thing that makes Belfast unique is the scenery surrounding it. So escape the city centre on New Years Day and get some fresh air. Take a trip up to the North Coast for the spectacular Giants Causeway. Tie up your walking boots and take on The Mourne Moutains in County Down. Or if you dont want to venture too far, Cave Hill sits right on Belfasts doorstep. If the steep climb doesnt take your breath away, the view of the city will.
