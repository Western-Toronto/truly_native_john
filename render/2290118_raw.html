BROADSPEED

Broadspeed was first established in 1963 as a car tuning and performance company. Ralph Broad formed Team Broadspeed competing within Group-3 of the British Touring Car Championship against the Ex-Works Mini Coopers. Even though the Broadspeed Minis were not always quicker in a straight line, they handled better and won many races. In the mid-60's Broadspeed moved its sights from BMC to Ford , campaigning Anglias and Escorts; John Fitzpatrick driving for Team Broadspeed won the British Saloon Car Championship in an Angila for 1966. By the mid 1970's, Broad firmly believed that Jaguar , the flagship of the BL group, should have an official racing presence and by 1977 he was ready to compete the Jaguar XJ 5.3 V12 Coupe which was, ultimately, dominated by the more reliable factory entered BMW 3.0 CSL coupes. The company was sold in 1977 to ex-Team Broadspeed racer John Handley; he operated the company as a Ford dealership in Sparkbrook, Birmingham. The Broadspeed forecourt was packed with new cars for sale - but naturally offering performance and tuning upgrades as well.
