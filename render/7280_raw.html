Meet Me in St Louis!

The birthplace of celebrated poet, Maya Angelou and the final resting place of author Tennessee Williams, St Louis has inspired writers through its 250-year history. Mark Twain, T.S. Eliot and more recently, Jonathan Franzen and Sally Benson (Meet Me in St Louis) are all native sons and daughters of the city that is often overlooked for neighboring Chicago. With the tallest monument (the Gateway Arch), the oldest botanical garden in the country, two downtown areas, and a City Museum thats really a playground for all ages, this is a destination city in its own right. While youre in town, stay at the historic and centrally located Omni Majestic Hotel, a 1913 restored building that is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. While in town, be sure to visit some or all of the following attractions.

When visiting the St Louis City Museum, be prepared for a serious adventure. It's almost indescribable, but leans toward a cross between Salvador Dali and Dr. Suess's surrealistic worlds. Housed in the 600,000 square foot building that was once the International Shoe Company, classically trained sculptor and internationally acclaimed artist, Bob Cassilly, and his creative crew of 20, sourced and salvaged building materials including old iron bridges, a school bus (which is precariously perched halfway off the roof of the museum), old chimneys, a ferris wheel, and two abandoned planes - and that's just the beginning. It's what they did with those materials that's really so amazing. For example, you could take the stairs when coming down from atop of this 11 -story building, or you could take the 10-story slide. There are treehouses, caves, tunnels, slides and places to climb and crawl for everyone. The best part is that big kids (yes, I'm talking parents) can have as much fun as the kids climbing and exploring. Get there early, there's a lot to see and do.

Admission after 5 p.m. Friday and Saturday $10

Mere photographs can't do the Gateway Arch justice. If you haven't seen it, you'll want to take a trip down to Laclede's Landing and visit it. You won't really appreciate just how tall the 630-foot arch - the tallest national monument in the nation - is until you're right up next to it. More than 900 hundred tons of stainless steel was used in the building of the arch - more than any other project in history. Take the 4-minute trip to the top of the arch in one of the egg-shaped 4-passenger pods and get a breathtaking view of Missouri and Illinois from the portals. You can enter the Gateway Arch for free. There's a charge for the trip to the top. Inside, at the foot of the arch, you'll find the interesting and informative Museum of Westward Expansion that pays tribute to the Lewis and Clark expedition and explains St Louis' role as 'gateway to the west' to early settlers, as well as a gift shop. There is plenty of parking available and the arch is easy to drive to; however, it's worth noting that MetroLink has a stop within a few hundred yards of the monument.

Founded in 1879, the stately Saint Louis Art Museum located in the heart of St Louis' expansive Forest Park strives to collect, present and conserve important and original works of art - paintings, sculptures, drawings and photographs - spanning 'five millennia and six continents' - with the intent to educate and inspire. Indeed, the spacious halls of this museum offer an excellent assortment of African American Art, Contemporary Art, Asian Art, and European Art to 1800, and so much more. As with many other St Louis attractions, admission is free; however, donations are appreciated. And, with the exception of Friday's, there may be a nominal charge for exhibitions.

Admission is free.

The Missouri Botanical Garden, a National Historic Landmark, is the oldest botanical garden in the United States and a world leader in botanical research. Founded in 1859 by Henry Shaw, the garden consists of 79 beautifully landscaped acres - including the tranquil 14-acre Japanese strolling garden, Henry Shaw's estate home, built in 1850 (open to the public), and one of the world's largest collections of rare and endangered orchids. Stroll through the fragrant rose garden, get lost in the garden's labyrinth, and go tropical in the half-acre Climatron. Hang out with the kids at the Children's Garden. The garden is a wonderfully relaxing and educational retreat from the hustle of city. Guided tours are available.

Some exhibits may require an additional charge. Extended hours (grounds only) Wednesdays and Saturdays, 7 to 9 a.m. Memorial Day - Labor Day, open Wednesdays, 5 to 8 p.m.

Once the largest and busiest passenger rail terminal in the world, St Louis Union Station, a National Historic Landmark, is now filled with more than 80 unique stores and restaurants - a veritable shopper's paradise. First opened in 1894, the station was closed in 1978. It opened, once again in 1985, as the 'largest adaptive re-use project in the nation.' Restaurants include The Hard Rock Caf, Landry's Seafood and the Mobil 4-star rated Station Grill. You'll also find the 539-room St Louis Union Station Marriott Hotel, recognized as the Historic Hotel of America. A visit to the Grand Hall and elegant ballrooms of yore will wane nostalgic. When you're ready for a break, relax by the lake on the plaza. MetroLink has a stop at Union Station, making this a very easy place to get to.

Located in the heart of downtown St. Louis, the historic Omni Majestic Hotel has been welcoming guests since 1913. The rooms have been renovated many times since construction, but the hotel preserves a historic 'Old St. Louis' character that brings back the image of the bustling city at the turn of the century. The hotel is just a short walk from most of the city's major attractions including the iconic Gateway Arch and the all-new Busch Stadium, the home of the St. Louis Cardinals. Enjoy classic pub fare at the hotel's beautiful Mahogany Grill with an all-day dining menu. WiFi internet is complimentary at the Omni Majestic Hotel for those who sign up for Omni's award-winning Select Guest program. Busy travelers will also appreciate the 24 hour room service menu and the 24 hour Business Center. For exploring the core of St. Louis, the Omni Majestic Hotel is the place to stay. TripAdvisor reviews consistently mention the hotel's friendly staff.
