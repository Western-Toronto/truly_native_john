Let The Sun In: Ford C-MAX


Its the first solar-powered car of its kind a plug-in hybrid vehicle that doesnt depend on the electricity grid. Its the Ford C-MAX and its driving a shift in the way we look at auto technology .

How does the Ford C-MAX work?

Most solar cars are powered via a battery connected to an electrical unit. The Ford C-MAX is different.

Launched in January 2014, the innovative car features C-MAX Solar Energi Concept, which uses a solar panel roof to extract energy from a special concentrator lens. Think of the lens as a super duper magnifying glass, directing the suns UV light onto the roof of the car. It magnifies sunlight by eight times, tracking the sun across the sky from east to west.

Youll need an entire day of sunshine to match the performance offered by a grid-connect solar car. But by doing so, the cars yearly greenhouse gas emissions are cut by four metric tons per average driver. Thats a huge saving.

The Ford C-MAX solar panels

The C-MAX really makes the most of solar technology. Ford turned to SunPower its long-standing technology partner to develop the incredibly efficient roof solar panels.

It then pulled in the team at Georgia Institute of Technology to come up with a way to amplify the suns energy, so that the car could be used by the everyday user. This was important, given the car takes longer to charge than other solar cars.

Driving off the grid

The beauty of the C-MAX is that it is entirely self-sufficient. Ford says up to 75 percent of trips could be entirely powered by the sun.

Ford C-MAX Solar Energi Concept shines a new light on electric transportation and renewable energy. As an innovation leader, we want to further the public dialog about the art of the possible in moving the world toward a cleaner future, said Mike Tinskey, Ford global director of vehicle electrification and infrastructure in a Ford press release.

The solar-powered plug-in hybrid electric car is certainly making waves for all the right resons.

C-MAX on the road

Since its unveiling, the C-MAX has been put through its paces. Ford and Georgia Institute of Technology have begun testing the car in a series of real-world scenarios.

Ford will then decide if the car can crack it on the road, and be sold on the market.

Heres hoping. Solar-powered cars are long overdue; and the sooner consumers can hop in a solar-powered vehicle, the better!
