Email blasts giving way to more personalized methods

By Michael McEnaney on Nov 06, 2014

Weve all heard the expression quality over quantity countless times in our lives, and while the email blast certainly had its day in the marketing sun, it appears as though that ship has sailed.

During the ad:tech New York eRetail Session #4," The Death of Email Blasts," on Day Two, it was clear the assembled panel of experts agreed the term email blast is taking marketing back to a time when truly connecting to ones audience wasnt seen as all that valuable. It was also a time when ad campaigns of every format were conducted with a one-size-fits-all shotgun approach. "Send it up the flagpole and see who salutes it" was the marketing mantra of the day.

Well, that was then, and this is the far more personalized now.

The sessions panel consisted of three veteran digital marketing experts: Rob Graham, CEO at Trainingcraft ; Dave Hendricks, president of LiveIntent and Bill McCloskey, the founder of Only Influencers . The trio wasted little time directing attendees attention to the one-on-one communication world they need to operate in today.

By the time these gentlemen were finished, the message had come through loud and clear: Its time to end the use of the email blast. Each of the email marketing gurus spoke to focusing future efforts on the quality and relevance of the messages being sent to customers, stressing that this is far more important and more valuable than focusing on the quantity of messages sent and the total number of consumers that receive them.

Each example illustrated the key today is to focus more on targeted messages that effectively and cleverly leverage your customer data and less on the total size of your audience.

Two of the more interesting email campaign ideas presented focused on the use of triggered and drip email methods. The panel explained that drip email campaigns are delivered based on pre-determined time intervals and are targeted more on delivering information precisely when the customer needs it, while trigger email marketing campaigns are initiated based more specifically on current and even past customer behavior and how that should trigger the senders response.

So, while the email blast may be riding off into the sunset, email marketing in general is clearly an old dog that has been taught some new tricks.
