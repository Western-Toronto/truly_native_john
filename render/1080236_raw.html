A Guide to the Best Gaming Headsets

Although graphics and gameplay have evolved considerably in the past few decades, sound has experienced its share of growth as well. Not that there was anything wrong with yesterdays catchy chiptune soundtracks, but todays technology allows sound to create gripping atmospheres for our virtual worlds like never before.

Its no surprise, then, that headsets have become an important purchase for most gamers.Especially in this day and age where the best type of gaming televisions and various electronic devices can all play their own sounds in the same living room. Headsets can help you hear everything clearly, no matter how noisy your surroundings.

For the online gamer, headsets arent a convenience but a must-have. They are the only way you can listen to your teammates in competitive modes to stay organized, warn others of enemy attacks, etc. Or, if you simply want to chat with friends while venturing through a multiplayer game, headsets are perfect; otherwise, youre stuck with typing to each other.

But with so many choices now available, how do you decide which headset to purchase? From speaker size and memory foam earcups to detachable mics and more, there are now a number of features to look for before making your decision.

That is why weve made a guide that goes over the important factors to consider when deciding on the right gaming headset for you.

To make your search even easier, we even have a few of the best gaming headsets we recommend under each categoryjust in case that feature is the most important to you!

Headsets with great comfort

If youre going to have something attached to your head for several hours a day, it may as well feel comfortable. Sound quality is important, but theres a reason comfort is the first thing you should consider when choosing a headset. By that, we mean who cares how nice they sound if wearing them hurts after an hour or two?

Obviously, the best way to make sure a headset feels good on your head is to actually wear it yourself, most likely at a store. But if this isnt an option because you want to buy online, pay attention to the headband sizing and make sure its adjustable. Whether your head is small, average size, or George Lopez status, an adjustable band will usually allow for a comfortable fit.

Cushioned ear cups are also a must-have, especially if youre the kind of person who gets irritated easily when wearing headsets of any kind. Most headsets today come with foam cushions or replaceable cushion pads so your melon doesnt feel like its having the juice squeezed out of it when you wear them.

The size of a headset can also play a role in comfort, specifically, the weight of the headset. It makes sense that headaches and neck fatigue are more likely when youre forced to wear a heavier object for one to several hours at a time. This one may not be as big of a deal as adjustable bands or cushioned cups, but its worth mentioning nonetheless.

For the gamer who pulls all-nighters or who simply wants a headset that provides maximum comfort, here are a few good choices.

Its no surprise that one of the most popular wireless gaming headsets in recent years also happens to receive praise for its comfort. Logitechs $100 headsets come with both cushioned ear pads and adjustable headbands made of memory foam. Add to that a light weight and youve got yourself a headset youll forget youre wearing, even after hours of game time.

Despite looking pretty big, numerous customer reviews praise how lightweight these headsets are for their size . The SteelSeries 9H also boast high-quality memory foam on both the ear cushions and adjustable headbands, allowing you to wear them for long periods of time without any strain.

This headset is the way to go if you plan to use it for not only gaming, but music, movies, and other entertainment.

The material also ensures that theyll offer more durability, which means you wont have to worry about buying replacement pads.

Gaming headsets with great sound quality

The ability to clearly hear enemy footsteps behind you, emotion-heightening music, or the voices of other players has become essential for the modern gamer. That is why for many of you reading this guide, finding a gaming headset which produces the best audio possible is #1 on your list of priorities.

Of course, theres plenty that goes into what makes a headset sound good, but it also depends on what you want. Some gamers prefer 7.1 channel surround sound while others go with stereo game sound. Which one is better mostly depends on the types of games you plan to play.

Surround sound headsets mean there are several speakers in the cups, allowing you to more easily recognize where a noise is coming from. So if youre playing a shooter game and an enemy tosses a grenade a few feet to your left, youll know its to your left via the sound coming from that direction. Thus, surround sound is preferred for online multiplayer gamers.

So why even consider stereo sound? Unfortunately, a surround sound headset can experience a slight distortion in sound quality since its being produced through several speakers. So if you want to near-flawless sound, stereo might be the way to go.

Youll also notice that gaming headset speakers generally come in two sizes: 40mm and 50mm. Headsets with 50mm speakers are bigger and thus known for offering better bass, although the size and shape of a headset also plays a role. In other words, a 40mm speaker headset can often produce just as good a quality of sound as a 50mm headset; it all depends on the design and material quality.

Lastly, an open versus closed design can also play a part in sound quality, especially if hearing the slightest detail is important to you. With an open design, you may miss an in-game sound due to a noise coming from your surroundings, but these are less likely to make your ears hot and sweaty. On the other hand, closed gaming headsets make sure nothing disturbs your audio experience, but they can be less effective at keeping your ears cool.

Microphone quality for gaming

Whether youre playing a cooperative MMO like Guild Wars 2, a competitive shooter like Call of Duty, or any online multiplayer game, being able to communicate vocally with other players is not just a convenience but oftentimes a necessity. Theres no faster way of exchanging vital game information or just having a conversation, and for that you need a headset with a mic.

Naturally youll want a mic that delivers crystal clear reception so friends and teammates can hear you loud and clear. Truth is: you dont want to be that one player who no one can understand and eventually gets muted for sounding like youre gargling nails. Or worse, you dont want to own a terrible headset that causes everyone to hear a loud, piercing sound whenever you speak.

Typically you want a headset that offers noise cancelling features to lower the chance of other players hearing static when you talk. This also makes it less likely for noises going on around you to go through the mic, which can prove annoying to other players. Weve all played with that one kid whose mom we can hear yelling in the background.

An adjustable or detachable mic might also be of interest if you prefer not having it in front of your mouth all the time, such as when youre playing by yourself. This is mostly a convenience factor, but it can make all the difference if your video game time is split evenly between playing solo and with others.

Here are a couple great gaming headsets that are purchased mainly for their high microphone quality and/or features:

Mad Catz offers an amazing gaming headset with advanced noise cancellation that eliminates ambient sounds so your voice isnt distorted. The mic is detachable so it doesnt get in the way while youre playing solo, and it comes with illuminated mute notification so you can clearly know if mute is on or off. These come in four colors and arent exactly cheap, but what did you expect from a surround sound headset boasting a myriad of awesome features?

Not only does the mic only pick up voice, but it can also be moved out of the way, automatically muting upon adjustment. Customizable one-touch controls also let you manipulate the sound of voice, in-game sound, and more, giving you a convenient way to improve what you hear.

The voice pickup is so effective that voice-activated applications have been proven to work better with these headsets, which means youll be heard loud and clear no matter what game youre playing. The noise-cancelling dual microphone array also serves to provide clear communication in even the noisiest environments.

Gaming headsets for multiple platforms

In an ideal world, every headset on the market would be compatible with every electronic device we own. Sadly, thats not the case, so choosing the right headset can depend entirely on which video game platform it supports.

The good news? Many headsets that dont necessarily advertise being compatible with certain consoles actually are. The bad news is that many only work for one or two platforms and not the others. For example, there are a good number of wireless headsets designed for PC and PS3 that are useless on Xbox 360 when it comes to chat functionality.

To help make your search for a widely-compatible headset a bit better, here are a couple of headsets that support at least two or more platforms, be it PC, MAC, or video game consoles from the two latest generations:

1. HyperX Cloud Gaming Headset

Kingstons amazing gaming headset makes it into another category for yet another great reason. The HyperX Cloud is one of few headsets that is compatible with almost every console and device , including: PS4, Xbox One (Stereo Adapter Required), MAC, PC, and iOS devices. Several customer reviews suggest ways of making it compatible with PS3 and Xbox 360 by using a USB adapter and changing the output settings, while others claim they used a 3.5mm jack to dual-RCA jacks female/female adapter.

2. Turtle Beach Ear Force PX22 Amplified Gaming Headset

According to the product page, Turtle Beachs Ear Force PX22 can be used with several consoles and devices. These include MAC, PS3, Xbox 360, PC, as well as tablets and smartphones. However, this headset was released in September of 2013, before Xbox One and PS4 released. This is notable because a great amount of comments suggest this headset is compatible with the two mentioned new-gen consoles as well. Note that apparently they only work as an audio headset on the Xbox One, which means you wont be able to use the mic for chat features.

3. Logitech Wireless Gaming Headset G930

One of the more popular gaming platform combinations today is the PS4 and PC. If you fit this description, Logitechs G930 is perfect as its compatible with Sonys latest consoles as well as PCs with Windows 8, Windows 7, and even Windows Vista. Keep in mind that various customer comments verify that this headset DOES NOT work with either Xbox One or Xbox 360 consoles.

4. Sennheiser PC 310 Gaming Headset

These German-designed headsets are confirmed to work on several platforms , including PC, PS4, Wii U, 3DS, and PS Vita. Keep in mind that an adapter is required to use the microphone for PS4; otherwise, you can only hear sound and cannot do any chat features. At less than $50, these headsets are a steal when you consider that they support several platforms.

5. Turtle Beach Ear Force XP300 Wireless Gaming Headset

These may cost nearly $200, but thats because they support just about every non-Nintendo console and platform . This includes PC, last gen consoles like PS3 and Xbox 360, as well as todays Xbox One and PS4. Note that for Xbox One its audio only, and unfortunately we cannot confirm if there is an adapter that allows for voice and chatting functionality.

Other factors to consider in a gaming headset

Gaming headset durability

Even if a cheaper headset offers excellent audio quality, chances are its made of less-durable material and thus has a shorter lifespan. If you expect to have a gaming headset for many years to come, make sure its made with top quality materials.

More importantly, carefully look at customer reviews on Amazon and other sites which mention headsets that suffered from low sound quality or comfort after a certain time of use. It goes without saying that the higher-priced headsets tend to be the most durable and long-lasting ones on the market.

Wireless or wired gaming headphones?

Wireless headsets are great for gamers that prefer not to have cords twisting and hanging all over them. Believe it or not, getting distracted by tangled headset wires can spell the difference between surviving a confrontation or getting killed. Needless to say, you dont have to worry about this with a wireless headset, but they often tend to cost more.

Note that most wireless headsets require a USB port to charge and that the average headset offers 10 hours of gameplay when fully charged. Although usually more expensive, some headsets have Bluetooth technology and boast a range of several dozen feet, which means you can keep chatting and listening when you exit the room or raid the fridge.

Programmable buttons

Just like a gaming mouse, there are headsets that come with buttons you can set specific controls to. For headsets, this includes setting a button to adjust game sound, voice chat volume, and other features.

Keep in mind that not too many headsets come with programmable buttons yet, which is fine because even the most hardcore gamers can go without them. But if youre really interested, check out Logitechs G930 gaming headset, which made a few appearances in our above categories.
